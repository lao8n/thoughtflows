# Systems map — the dynamical model (loops, couplings, branches)

*Replaces the spine with a dynamical read: feedback loops (tagged empirical/theoretical, with a live gauge + flip-threshold), the coupling map that makes them one system, and the discrete branches + theory-templates that give the decade its shape. Prediction = shape + weighted branches + watch-thresholds, not point forecasts.*

**Dominant dynamic:** The master-clock contest at GV11: the RSI reinforcing loop (R1) pushing capability toward a self-closing, field-freezing takeoff versus the diffusion/commoditization flywheel (R3) pushing that same capability out into open weights that erase every lead. Diffusion is winning now — every RSI gauge reads sub-unity and stuck at long horizons while every diffusion gauge compounds — so the decisive question is purely one of timing: does a decisive-capability threshold arrive before commoditization completes (pre-2032), or does the diffusion clock run the table.

**System-dominant loop:** R2 — the compute-capex flywheel. It is the engine literally moving trillions right now (capex +63/73% YoY, NVIDIA DC revenue ~38x, CoWoS ~10x) and every other loop is currently reacting to the buildout it drives; R3 (diffusion) is its pervasive erosive counter-force, but R2 is what sets the system's tempo and stakes today.

**Current phase:** Perez late frenzy / installation approaching the turning-point test, nested inside a Dalio late-top / early-decline reserve-currency phase (fiscal-dominance drift), with the Bostrom clock still pre-crossover (RSI loop open). A financial-capital mania on expected ROI, running into a power/grid ceiling and a demand-validation reckoning.

**Macro trajectory (the shape):** A frenzy-phase capex mania colliding with two hard ceilings — power/grid execution (B2) and demand/ROI validation (B1) — while the diffusion clock relentlessly commoditizes the model layer and pushes durable rent up-stack toward power, compute, cloud, and the reorganized application layer. The modal shape is a turbulent turning-point wobble (partial shakeout, not clean takeoff nor clean deployment) resolving into a messy, multipolar deployment under soft financial repression, with the physical-frontier winner frequently NOT the value winner. Overhanging this base arc are two discrete detonators — a Taiwan fracture that zeroes both poles at once, and (quarantined, never averaged in) an RSI takeoff that would freeze the field into winner-take-all.

**Synthesis:** The systems model says this is not a single race with one winner but a chokepoint lattice running against two clocks under a late-cycle monetary envelope. The slow Dalio boundary condition (fiscal-dominance drift, contested second-pole) sets the cost of capital and the geopolitical stakes; the medium Perez cycle (R2 capex flywheel now dominant, B1 fragility latent) sets the tempo and the coming turning-point test; the fast Bostrom loop (R1) is quarantined and open. These nest — the outer bounds the inner — and must NOT be averaged. The dominant contest is R1 vs R3 at GV11, and R3 (diffusion) is winning decisively today, which is why the modal world (A, ~0.55) is messy multipolar deployment in which durable rent migrates up-stack to power/compute/cloud/apps and the physical-frontier holder is often not the value winner. Because leading-edge chips bind training but not inference, because export controls tighten on hardware while leaking on outcomes, and because power is the true US binding stave taxed by China's mineral lever, no actor holds an uncontested path: security-denial at the frontier can succeed while the paradigm still 'fails' on diffusion, commercial value-capture, and labor reinstatement simultaneously. The forecast is therefore a shape plus quarantined tails, not a point: base-case up-stack deployment under soft repression (0.55), a Perez ROI bust (0.20), a Taiwan fracture that zeroes both poles (0.15), and — held strictly apart — the low-probability, unbounded-payoff RSI takeoff (0.10). Watch the RE-bench long-horizon crossing and the revenue-per-token/amortization gate above all else: the first switches on the quarantined world, the second tips the base case into the bust.

## Branches (discrete regimes)

### A · Base case — diffusion-deployment, no decisive threshold — **P=0.55**
- trigger: GV11 crosses no decisive-capability threshold before ~2032; diffusion clock (R3) outruns every moat; controls hold at the frontier but leak on outcomes; power ceiling (B2) throttles but does not break the buildout.
- active loops: R3, R2, B2, B3, R4, B4, B5
- shape: Perez frenzy matures through a turbulent-but-survivable turning-point into a messy multipolar deployment. Value migrates relentlessly up-stack; chip<->mineral MAD standoff holds; monetary system drifts into soft financial repression as the productivity offset arrives late (B2 starves GV40).
- who wins: Distributed and contested: owners of power, compute, cloud aggregation, and the reorganized application layer capture durable rent — NOT necessarily the frontier-fabrication or frontier-model holder. No uncontested path for any single actor; open-weight capability floor persists.

### C · Buildout bust — Perez turning-point / ROI reckoning — **P=0.2**
- trigger: Revenue-per-token stays below infra amortization at scale for ~2-3 sustained quarters (GV14 miss) -> neocloud/SPV impairment cascade transmits through circular vendor financing to the core capex plan.
- active loops: B1, B2, R2, B4
- shape: Frenzy flips into turning-point crash a la telecom/fiber 2000-02: SPV-tier impairment marks down GPU collateral, capex retrenches, frontier training starves. Bust is followed — Perez-style — by a deployment golden age on cheap over-built capacity. Can co-detonate B4 via a growth/credit shock.
- who wins: Survivors with balance-sheet insulation (hyperscalers on $50B+ FCF) and distressed-asset buyers; frontier labs dependent on external capital and the neocloud/SPV tier are wiped. Value locus shifts down toward end-users on cheapened compute.

### D · Exogenous fracture — Taiwan / kinetic shock — **P=0.15**
- trigger: Taiwan/TSMC (GV21) discontinuity — kinetic conflict, blockade, or fab-offline event — and/or full mutual chip<->mineral escalation past the MAD deterrent.
- active loops: R4, B3, B4
- shape: The one discontinuity that zeroes both poles at once: leading-edge supply and mineral/grid supply both cut, monetary layer detonated directly (B4 plumbing break -> forced Fed backstop). Rapid forced decoupling, capex freeze, regime-change in the monetary order.
- who wins: Nobody cleanly; relative winners are whoever holds reshored/redundant fabrication, mineral processing, and grid capacity. Catastrophic for the frontier race on both sides; accelerates the Dalio external-conflict phase.

### B · RSI takeoff — decisive strategic advantage (THEORETICAL, quarantined) — **P=0.1** · *theoretical/quarantined*
- trigger: AI matches/exceeds human experts on sustained 32h+ frontier ML-R&D horizons (METR RE-bench crosses unity) BEFORE ~2032 — a regime not yet entered.
- active loops: R1, R2, R4
- shape: Loop gain crosses unity; R1 self-closes and compounds endogenously. Diffusion (R3) is frozen out as capability outruns commoditization; R2 and R4 lock into winner-take-all. Recalcitrance -> crossover -> fast takeoff -> singleton.
- who wins: The lab / nation holding the lead THROUGH the threshold — unbridgeable and winner-take-all. Its loops never touch the base case; treat as a separate quarantined world, not a probability-averaged input.

## Theory-templates (nested by time-scale)

### Dalio Big Cycle (empire / debt / reserve-currency) _(slow)_
- governs: The outermost boundary condition: the monetary-fiscal substrate (B4) and the second-pole / reserve-currency contest (GV27, GV36-42). Sets the macro envelope every faster loop runs inside — the cost of capital, the credibility of the marginal UST bid, and the geopolitical stakes of the frontier race.
- phases: Rise (sound money, productivity, rising empire) → Top / peak (debt bubble, wealth gaps, financialization, challenger rises) → Decline (fiscal dominance, monetization/repression, internal + external conflict) → New order / restructuring
- where we are: Late-top / early-decline for the incumbent reserve power: debt-service and AI+rearmament capex raise r*, the sovereign UST bid shrinks, and the system drifts toward soft financial repression (B4). A credible challenger (China) is rising but not yet a credible second pole (GV27 contested).
- base-rate shape: Multi-decade arc that resolves fiscal excess through inflate-away / financial repression rather than default; reserve status erodes slowly with a strong floor unless a plumbing break or war forces a discrete regime change.

### Perez techno-economic surge / financial-capital cycle _(medium)_
- governs: The tempo of the compute-capex buildout and its diffusion: R2 (capex flywheel), B1 (capital fragility), R3 (commoditization/diffusion), GV15 value-locus. Nested inside the Dalio envelope — the surge is being financed under late-cycle monetary conditions.
- phases: Irruption (new general-purpose tech appears) → Frenzy / installation (financial-capital mania, over-build, decoupling from fundamentals) → Turning point (crash / shakeout — the ROI reckoning) → Synergy / deployment (production-capital golden age, broad diffusion) → Maturity
- where we are: Late frenzy / installation: hyperscaler capex +63/73% YoY, NVIDIA DC revenue ~38x, CoWoS ~10x — a classic financial-capital mania running on expected not realized ROI, approaching the turning-point test (GV14 demand validation).
- base-rate shape: Installation frenzy over-builds physical capacity, hits a turning-point crash (railways 1840s, telecom/fiber 2000-02), then the cheap over-built capacity powers a longer deployment golden age. Bust is modal-but-not-certain; deployment is near-certain eventually.

### Acemoglu-Restrepo task displacement vs reinstatement _(medium)_
- governs: The labor / demand base: B5 (reinstatement / underconsumption brake), GV17 reinstatement ratio, GV18 wage-share. Runs on a diffusion-lagged clock (GV13) inside the Perez deployment phase.
- phases: Displacement (automation substitutes existing tasks) → Immiseration interlude (labor share falls, lagged before new tasks appear) → Reinstatement (new labor-complementary tasks created) → New equilibrium or persistent polarization
- where we are: Early displacement: entry-level knowledge-work contraction and apprenticeship-severance signals, but no clear net destruction yet — buffered by the deployment lag (GV13). Reinstatement ratio indeterminate.
- base-rate shape: Historically reinstatement arrives but with 15-30yr lags and multi-decade immiseration interludes (textiles, farm, factory). The brake bites only if displacement outruns reinstatement AND the consumer-credit buffer exhausts.

### Bostrom RSI loop-gain / decisive strategic advantage _(fast)_
- governs: The innermost, fastest loop: R1 (recursive self-improvement) and its coupling to GV11. Fires inside — and only if permitted by — the Perez buildout and Dalio envelope. Quarantined: its dynamics do not touch the base case unless the crossover threshold is crossed.
- phases: Recalcitrance / slow (loop gain sub-unity, human-paced R&D) → Crossover (AI matches human expert at long-horizon R&D) → Fast takeoff (endogenous compounding) → Singleton / decisive strategic advantage
- where we are: Pre-crossover, loop open and sub-unity: AI ~4x humans at 2h but ~0.5x at 32h ML-R&D; SWE-bench flattening 75->81%. Automation buys speed, not depth. The loop is not closed.
- base-rate shape: Reference-class-free and bimodal: either the loop stays open and fizzles into the diffusion regime, or gain crosses unity and compounds hard into a winner-take-all takeoff. No smooth middle.

- **The master capability clock (RSI vs diffusion)** (R1, R3): Diffusion (R3) dominant and accelerating (efficiency halving ~8mo, open-weight lag 2yr->3mo, price -40x/yr); RSI (R1) open and sub-unity at long horizons. - **Labor / aggregate-demand base** (B5): Latent and indeterminate underconsumption brake: early displacement signals but reinstatement ratio not yet clearly <1, and the consumer-credit buffer not yet exhausted. Buffered by the diffusion lag; the slow-fuse check on whether the buildout starves its own demand.
