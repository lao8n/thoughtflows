## Cross-gate read

The gates split along a control/decisiveness axis, and that split is the thesis. The gates the US/allies CONTROL — leading-edge fabs (GV1) and EUV/EDA tooling (GV2) — are the structurally hardest to close and are holding: SMIC's node cadence is decelerating into a DUV yield wall while TSMC's frontier keeps moving, so the USABLE ~2-node gap is stuck-open through 2030. But the gates that decide OUTCOMES — efficiency/diffusion (GV10) and export-control BITE (GV4) — are closing fast, and the single meta-crux (GV11's long-horizon RSI-depth on RE-Bench) is the one derivative that is FLAT, not accelerating. So the moats hold while the advantage they exist to protect diffuses on a 6-18-month clock: controlled chokepoints buy delay, not dominance. Meanwhile the US's OWN binding constraint is the one gate it cannot clear with money — power/grid (GV6), widening on transformers and transmission while China adds ~8x US net capacity — so deployment, not capability, is the axis where the asymmetry runs AGAINST the US, with minerals (GV8) a holstered tax on top. The pattern implies the US leads frontier TRAINING throughout but conditionally (on Taiwan quiet + RSI shallow), while losing the DEPLOYMENT/diffusion race structurally: a multipolar, commoditized base case in which the front-loaded 2026-29 window is the only period the US lead is decisive.

**Boldest departures from consensus (fall out of the trends):**
- Export controls will be tacitly recognized as a strategic FAILURE by ~2028 — the GV4 derivatives (reversing revenue share, accelerating smuggling ~1/3 of China compute, Ascend scaling) show effective denial decaying from a wall to a 1.5-2.5yr delay; the consensus 'controls preserve the US lead' premise inverts to 'controls bought a depreciating durational lead the US failed to monetize.'
- The binding US AI constraint flips from CHIPS to ELECTRONS by 2027-28: GV6 (widening transformer/transmission, stuck HV lines) forces the frontier behind-the-meter (gas/nuclear/SMR) and pushes MARGINAL inference OFFSHORE — making the Gulf a genuine swing inference-supplier with real leverage over where compute physically lives by 2030, not merely a landlord.
- The AGI winner-take-all bet LOSES: ~65-70% plateau/fast-diffusion base case through 2030 because the one non-accelerating metric (GV11 RE-Bench long-horizon crossover — 'speed not depth') is the deciding variable. This directly undercuts the framing that underwrites export-control and Stargate-scale capex.
- The true China compute ceiling is HBM, not wafers or nodes — CXMT stack output (caps Ascend at ~250-300k/yr vs a 600k target) governs China's frontier-inference scale more than SMIC yield does; the whole 'nodes behind' framing watches the wrong bottleneck.
- NVIDIA's monopoly-grade ~73% data-center margin begins a visible compression toward ~65-68% before 2028 as custom inference silicon eats share — the single most valuable rent locus starts NORMALIZING even as the aggregate compute bill keeps rising (Jevons), a timing call most treat as years away.
- A NON-kinetic Taiwan scare (blockade/quarantine drill, ~15-20% by 2028) is materially more probable than invasion AND is the actual disruptor — and structural risk is HIGHER late-decade even as deterrence nominally 'holds,' because diversification thins the silicon shield faster than it de-risks; a brief event alone spikes global compute prices.
- Pure model-layer independence is dead by ~2028: frontier labs (OpenAI/Anthropic) survive only as compute-demand subsidiaries of cloud parents or by integrating up-stack — durable rent locks at hardware+cloud, and the EU 10^25-FLOP threshold plus the export-control premise are both quietly rendered unenforceable by a jurisdiction-independent open-weight capability floor.
- China's rare-earth/minerals lever delivers its maximum value by staying HOLSTERED — MAD-with-chips makes it a bargaining chip whose every use accelerates the substitution that erodes it; the consensus 'China's devastating counter-lever' is really a self-limiting +15-25% tax the US converts to a managed cost over 5-10yr.

**Why it's soft_slow:** The fab step itself is a slow cost/time tax rather than a physical wall — SMIC has PROVEN sub-7nm is reachable on DUV. But the gate is hard-gated from behind by GV2 (EUV): sub-3nm at economic yield requires ASML EUV (single Dutch source, ~$150-200M/tool, ~100k components from ~800 suppliers, plus Zeiss sub-atomic mirrors and tin-plasma sources), which China cannot buy or build. On DUV, each node step multiplies exposure/etch passes (7nm needs ~34 DUV exposures vs EUV's 9), which compounds defect probability and inflates cost 2-3x while capping throughput. So the binding constraint is (a) tacit process-recipe knowledge — never transferred to a greenfield entrant since 1987 — and (b) the EUV tool monopoly. Money and DUV workarounds get China to ~5nm-class density at ~20% yield; the wall is 'economic sub-3nm', which is genuinely hard.

**Why the trend moved this way:** China closed 14nm->7nm->5nm-class faster than the '10-year-behind' consensus by brute-forcing DUV multipatterning on ASML NXT:1980-class immersion scanners stockpiled before controls tightened, backed by ~$49B/yr state-directed equipment spend and repatriated TSMC/Samsung talent — NOT by acquiring EUV. That path has a hard ceiling: multipatterning stacks more litho/etch cycles per layer, so as SMIC pushed from 7nm (~34 exposures, <50% yield) to 5nm N+3 (~20% yield, 2-3x TSMC's cost), the economics degraded faster than the density improved. Meanwhile TSMC advanced ~1 node/2yr on EUV (5nm 2020 -> 3nm 2022 -> 2nm HVM 2025) at healthy yields, so the *usable* gap has NOT closed even as the nominal-nm gap narrowed to ~2 nodes. The real near-term supply ceiling for frontier accelerators is not wafers but CoWoS packaging (GV3), which TSMC scaled ~10x (13k->130k wpm) in three years to chase AI demand.

**Driving factors (past → future):**
- **EUV access (GV2 gates GV1)** — *past:* Its ABSENCE forced China onto DUV multipatterning, which still delivered 7nm/5nm-class density — the gap closed fast precisely because DUV was 'good enough' down to ~5nm. *→ future:* The next leg (economic sub-3nm) REQUIRES EUV. This is where the trend stalls: without ASML High-NA/EUV, SMIC's DUV path hits a yield/cost wall around 5nm-class. The catch-up rate that looked linear inflects sharply upward in difficulty at the 3nm boundary.
- **Yield/cost economics of DUV multipatterning** — *past:* Enabled indigenized 7nm at low-but-usable yield for prestige/military volumes (Kirin, Ascend 910B/C), converting political denial into partial capability. *→ future:* At ~20% yield and 2-3x cost, 5nm N+3 cannot sustain the WAFER VOLUMES a frontier training build-out needs. Governs whether China can build large dense clusters (no) vs. boutique quantities (yes). Yield-learning may lift 5nm over 2-3yr but never to EUV-cost parity.
- **State capital + talent repatriation (the CATL-playbook test)** — *past:* ~$49B/yr equipment spend (largest global market, +35% in 2024) and ex-TSMC engineers compressed the timeline vs. organic pace. *→ future:* Capital has plateaued (~$49B, flat 2025) because the binding input is now tool ACCESS not money — you cannot buy what's export-controlled. Analogy discipline: the Nikon/turbine-blade cases say money+talent yields partial, not parity, capability against a tacit-knowledge + equipment-monopoly frontier.
- **Geographic diversification vs. the risk-window clock** — *past:* TSMC Arizona went 0 -> ~95k wpm 4nm (2022-2025) with Taiwan-parity yields, proving onshore leading-edge is feasible. *→ future:* But onshore 2nm only ~2028-29 — the diversification matures AFTER the 2027-30 Taiwan risk window. Governs the US SPOF exposure: for the next ~3-4yr the frontier remains Taiwan-concentrated (>90%), so a Taiwan discontinuity still resets leading-edge to near-zero for ALL parties.

**Historical analogies:**
- **Nikon/Canon vs. ASML photolithography failure, 1984-2020** (corpus): Nikon held ~50-55% of leading-edge steppers by the mid-1990s; ASML entered as a minor player and overtook it, capturing the entire EUV node while Nikon retained only mature-node and adjacent-equipment positions. — *implies here:* Predicts China achieves partial equipment/fab capability at mature-to-midrange nodes while remaining shut out of the EUV-dependent frontier — a bifurcation, not parity. Breaks because Nikon lost in an OPEN market with no export controls; China faces controls PLUS the ecosystem gap, making its path structurally harder on two axes at once.
- **GE/P&W/Rolls-Royce single-crystal turbine-blade oligopoly, 1960s-present** (corpus): Single-crystal superalloy blade casting stayed a 3-firm tacit-knowledge monopoly for decades; China's WS-15 program reached partial (not parity) capability only after ~30 years. — *implies here:* Calibrates the catch-up clock for complex tacit-knowledge manufacturing: expect China to reach partial indigenous sub-5nm capability on a multi-decade, not multi-year, timeline — and 'partial' (low yield, non-frontier) is the realistic ceiling, matching SMIC's ~20%-yield 5nm. Breaks because chips have open-weight/algorithmic substitution (GV10) with no turbine-blade equivalent — denial converts to delay more readily than in jet engines.
- **ASML EUV lithography monopoly, 2012-present** (corpus): 25+ years of R&D produced a single global EUV supplier; a determined, well-funded China has been unable to replicate it despite billions in spend and espionage. — *implies here:* The strongest contemporary case that a true industrial chokepoint HOLDS against a well-resourced adversary — supports the forward call that the EUV-gated sub-3nm wall persists through 2030. Breaks because ASML is a commercial firm (~$36B rev) with fiduciary/lobbying pressure to keep selling, and EUV is only ~13yr old — an unprecedentedly recent and complex monopoly whose durability at 20-50yr timescales is genuinely unproven.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERANTS (gate clears faster): a Chinese High-NA/EUV breakthrough or a working indigenous EUV source (SSMB/steady-state microbunching, tin-LPP) — low probability this decade but would be the single biggest inflection; a large-scale smuggling/grey-market route to EUV tools or parts; SMIC yield-learning lifting 5nm from ~20% toward ~50% (makes low-volume frontier chips economic); loosened controls under a political deal. STALLS/REVERSES (gate hardens): tightened controls on DUV service, spare parts, and sub-systems (chokes even the existing 7nm/5nm fleet); ASML/US blocking maintenance of installed immersion scanners; a Taiwan discontinuity (GV21) that resets everyone; deepening cost/yield wall as multipatterning steps become defect-dominated below 5nm. For the US SPOF: Arizona/Japan/Germany ramps accelerating would de-risk, while a CHIPS pullback or fab-delay widens the exposure window.

**Forward call:** 2028: SMIC produces 5nm-class (N+3) in low volume at improving-but-still-uneconomic yield (~30-40%), and may DEMO ~3nm on DUV, but not at usable volume; TSMC is in HVM at A14/1.4nm-class. The usable gap holds at ~2 nodes / ~4-5 years. TSMC Arizona at 3nm; onshore 2nm not yet. 2030: China remains capped at ~5nm-class economically without EUV; probability China indigenizes true economic sub-3nm at scale by 2030 ≈ 25-30% (contingent almost entirely on an EUV-source breakthrough). The gate stays in the 'US/allied-controlled monopoly, DUV-multipatterning ~5nm bifurcation ceiling for China' state through 2030 — HIGH confidence (~80%). US Taiwan-SPOF exposure remains elevated through the 2027-29 window before Arizona 2nm meaningfully diversifies (~2029-30).

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If the gate holds (China capped at ~5nm-class, low-yield, no EUV): China CANNOT out-TRAIN the US frontier — it lacks the dense, cheap, high-yield leading-edge wafers to build the largest training clusters, and pays a 2-3x $/transistor and higher power/chip penalty. Second-order, per-actor: (1) CHINA competes on a different axis — algorithmic efficiency, open-weight models (DeepSeek-style), inference-time scaling, and deploying on MATURE nodes + captured/smuggled H-class chips (GV10 substitutes for GV1). It builds 'adequate' AI (a Swiss-watch-style commodity/frontier bifurcation), not frontier-out-training AI. Ascend-class parts serve domestic inference but hit CUDA/interconnect penalties (GV5) that compound the fab gap into a 30-60% effective-cluster deficit. (2) US/allies retain the frontier-TRAINING monopoly and thus first-mover on any capability-threshold jump (GV11) — but remain hostage to the Taiwan SPOF: >90% of sub-5nm in one seismic/geopolitical chokepoint means a Taiwan halt resets the US's OWN frontier + defense compute to near-zero, and Arizona 2nm arrives too late to cover 2027-29. So the US 'wins' the fab race conditionally on Taiwan stability. (3) Frontier accelerator SUPPLY is throttled near-term by CoWoS/HBM (GV3), not wafers — so even the leader's scaling is packaging-bound until ~2026-27.

**…if it BREAKS:** If the gate BREAKS toward China (indigenous EUV source or economic sub-3nm at scale before 2030): the frontier-training denial collapses — China could build large dense clusters domestically, neutralizing the single most important US leverage point (export controls become theater), and the competition shifts entirely to power (GV) and algorithms where China has structural advantages. Price this at ~25-30% by 2030 and treat any credible indigenous-EUV/High-NA demonstration as the key early-warning tripwire. If instead the gate breaks the OTHER way for the US (a Taiwan discontinuity, GV21): leading-edge fab goes to near-zero for ALL parties simultaneously — a symmetric catastrophe that freezes the frontier globally, hands transient advantage to whoever holds the most stockpiled chips and onshore capacity (marginally the US via Arizona + Samsung/Intel), and makes the diversification-timing mismatch the decisive variable. Either break is a regime change, not a marginal move.

**Why it's hard_physical:** EUV is the hardest, most durable chokepoint in the compute stack because it fuses three near-irreproducible ecosystems: (1) Zeiss sub-atomic-flatness mirrors + metrology, a ~150-year Jena optics tradition; (2) a 250W+ tin-plasma (LPP) light source (Cymer/ASML) firing ~50,000 tin droplets/sec hit twice by CO2 lasers; (3) ~5,000 suppliers and decades of tacit integration knowledge that no single actor holds. On the design side, Cadence+Synopsys+Siemens hold ~74% of global EDA and >70% inside China; frontier design tools embed decades of foundry-co-optimized process knowledge. The optics ecosystem historically resisted even forcible full transfer (Zeiss under WWII occupation). This is a physical + tacit-knowledge wall, not a cost tax — though the workaround below (DUV multipatterning) turns the near-term into a cost/yield tax up to ~5nm.

**Why the trend moved this way:** Two trends run in opposite directions. (1) At the leading edge, ASML's EUV monopoly is effectively total and STUCK at ~100% of sub-5nm tooling — no competitor exists, and export controls have frozen China out of EUV entirely since 2019. (2) BELOW the gate, China has closed the node gap fast via a workaround: SMIC went 28nm(2015)→14nm(2019)→7nm(2023, Kirin 9000S)→5nm pilot(2025) NOT with EUV but by running repatriated ASML DUV-immersion (NXT:1980-series) scanners through self-aligned multipatterning — 34 exposure steps for 7nm where EUV needs 9. That path works but its ceiling is ~5nm: each extra node multiplies mask count, defectivity and cost (5nm SMIC yields ~20% and ~50% costlier than TSMC), so it stalls on economics before ~3nm, which physically needs EUV. Meanwhile China's TRUE escape — indigenous EUV — remains ~4+ years behind: a Shenzhen LDP (laser-induced discharge plasma) prototype validated a 13.5nm beam in Dec 2025 at only ~100-150W vs ASML's 250W+, with mass-production targeted 2028 (2030 realistic). China is closing the mature-equipment and node gaps quickly, but the leading-edge lithography source/optics gap is the one that resists.

**Driving factors (past → future):**
- **EUV light source + Zeiss optics ecosystem (the physical core)** — *past:* The reason the gate never eroded at the leading edge: ASML took ~20 years and ~€6bn+ (plus acquiring Cymer and integrating Zeiss) to make LPP EUV work; no one else — including Nikon/Canon with a head start — could. China was frozen out entirely from 2019, so it could not even start climbing the leading-edge curve. *→ future:* This binds the NEXT leg hardest. China's DUV-multipatterning workaround explains the fast 28→7→5nm run, but that path has a hard ceiling at ~5nm on cost/yield. Sub-3nm REQUIRES a high-power (250W+) domestic EUV source + Zeiss-class optics — exactly the capabilities the Dec-2025 LDP prototype is still ~half-powered and years from producing at yield. The gate gets HARDER precisely where China now needs it most.
- **Export-control regime + allied (NL/JP) coordination** — *past:* The 2019 EUV ban + Jan-2024 DUV restrictions defined the battlefield: China could buy DUV before the door shut (hence the ASML China-revenue surge to 33% and the stockpiling), then was cut off. Controls, not market competition, are what keep the gate shut — Nikon lost to ASML in an OPEN market; China faces controls ON TOP of the capability gap. *→ future:* COCOM base rate says multilateral controls erode over 15-30 yrs under commercial pressure and allied defection — EU/US China-dependence divergence is the crack. But the controls only need to hold the ~4-year window until either China indigenizes or the frontier moves on. Tightening (SME, subsystems, service bans) extends the tax; EU defection or a grand bargain would shorten it.
- **State capital + whole-of-nation mobilization (SiCarrier/Huawei, Big Fund III)** — *past:* Big Fund III (~$47bn, 2024) + 3,000+ researchers coordinated by Huawei/SiCarrier drove the mature-equipment self-sufficiency surge (25→35% in a year) and the LDP prototype. Repatriated talent + unlimited capital compressed the mature-node and non-litho toolchain catch-up dramatically. *→ future:* Capital accelerates everything EXCEPT the tacit-knowledge and optics bottleneck, which money buys slowly. Predicts continued fast gains at mature nodes and in etch/deposition/EDA, but a long, uncertain slog on EUV source power, optics flatness, and yield — the parts that took ASML decades of learning-by-doing that capital cannot instantly replicate.
- **The DUV-multipatterning cost/yield economics** — *past:* Self-aligned multipatterning on repurposed DUV-immersion tools is HOW SMIC hit 7nm (34 steps vs EUV's 9) and is piloting 5nm — the single mechanism behind the fast visible catch-up. *→ future:* This is a soft cost-tax that compounds: each node below 7nm multiplies masks, defect opportunities and cost (5nm ~20% yield, ~50% costlier than TSMC). It lets China SERVE demand at 7-5nm at a premium, but it cannot out-scale TSMC economics and cannot reach ~3nm. Governs whether China competes on efficiency/volume at mature-advanced nodes (yes) vs. frontier training-chip cost-parity (no).

**Historical analogies:**
- **Carl Zeiss / Schott Glass precision-optics ecosystem (Jena, 1846–present) & WWII forced-transfer failure** (corpus): A ~150-year co-evolved ecosystem in precision optical design, specialty glass and metrology became the indispensable substrate for lithography optics. Even forcible transfer under WWII occupation (Operation Overcast, deportations) failed to reconstitute the capability elsewhere — the tacit process knowledge did not travel. — *implies here:* Strongest empirical argument that the EUV OPTICAL precision ecosystem is China's single most durable bottleneck — more durable even than the EUV source. Predicts the optics/metrology piece of a domestic EUV tool is where China lags longest. Breaks because modern metrology (interferometry, AFM, synchrotron X-ray) can characterize surfaces to atomic levels, and a funded peacetime program with full documentation may transfer knowledge better than chaotic 1940s occupation — possibly compressing the historical 10-20yr catch-up.
- **Nikon/Canon vs ASML in EUV (Japan photolithography failure, 1984–2020)** (corpus): Nikon held ~50-55% of leading-edge steppers in the mid-1990s; ASML entered in 1984 as a minor Philips JV and overtook them, then won EUV outright while Nikon/Canon abandoned it. Nikon lost in an OPEN market with no export controls against it. — *implies here:* The most material demonstration that leading-edge litho cannot be closed by the CATL/mass-manufacturing playbook — it requires an ecosystem, not just capital and scale. China faces this SAME capability gap PLUS export controls, i.e. structurally harder on two dimensions. Breaks in that Japan retained strength in adjacent equipment (Tokyo Electron, Advantest, Shin-Etsu) — so China will likely achieve partial/mature-node equipment self-sufficiency (as the 35% figure shows) while staying gapped on the one critical tool.
- **COCOM dual-use export controls (1949–1994)** (corpus): 17 Western allies denied the Soviet bloc militarily significant tech (computers, machine tools, precision instruments). Had real teeth in the 1950s-70s, eroded through the 1980s under commercial pressure and allied defection, and dissolved in 1994. — *implies here:* The base rate for the export-control leg: multilateral regimes slow but do not stop adversary progress, and erode over 15-30 yrs as commercial incentives and allied divergence build. Predicts the EUV controls buy a finite window (~this decade), not permanent denial — consistent with the 2026-28 window in the prior. Breaks because today's allied coordination (BIS entity lists, Wassenaar, binding NL/JP deals) is tighter than COCOM AND the controlled item (a physical tool needing 5,000 suppliers) is far harder to smuggle than a model-weight file — so erosion may be slower than the COCOM base rate.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERANTS (gate erodes faster): (1) China's LDP prototype hits ~250W and demonstrates yield before 2028; (2) allied defection — EU/Netherlands or Japan carve-outs under commercial pressure (ASML's shrinking China revenue raises the incentive), the COCOM failure mode; (3) SSMB/steady-state-microbunching or a synchrotron-based source leapfrogs LPP; (4) talent-mobility leaks of Zeiss/ASML/Cymer tacit knowledge (the Slater vector). STALLS/REVERSES (gate holds harder): (1) US extends controls to subsystems, service, and DUV spares, degrading China's installed DUV base; (2) optics/metrology proves as intransferable as the Zeiss WWII case; (3) DUV-multipatterning economics collapse at 5nm yields, capping the workaround; (4) High-NA EUV (ASML, ~$350M/tool) moves the frontier to 2nm/1.4nm, widening the absolute gap even if China reaches standard EUV.

**Forward call:** By 2028: The leading-edge gate HOLDS. ASML remains ~100% of sub-5nm EUV tooling (>90% probability). China has a functioning indigenous EUV prototype but NOT yield-viable high-volume manufacturing — it stays capped at ~5nm in real volume via DUV multipatterning, at premium cost and low yield (~70% probability). Non-litho equipment and EDA self-sufficiency keep climbing (equipment >45%, EDA ~15-20%). By 2030: China achieves limited domestic EUV pilot production (the stated 2028 target slips to ~2030, ~55% probability) but at a fraction of TSMC/ASML throughput, yield and cost — enough for strategic/military volumes, not cost-competitive frontier commercial logic. The gate transitions from "hard wall" to "expensive, low-yield, 1-2 nodes behind" for China — never fully closed this decade. High-NA EUV keeps the Western frontier (2nm/1.4nm) moving so the ABSOLUTE gap barely narrows even as China reaches standard EUV.

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If the gate holds (base case), the downstream AI consequences are concrete and per-actor: CHINA cannot achieve cost-parity frontier TRAINING silicon this decade — its accelerators (Ascend-class) are built on 7-5nm DUV-multipatterned dies at ~20% yield and ~50% cost premium, meaning higher $/token and higher power-per-FLOP than TSMC-made Nvidia/Google parts. So China rationally competes NOT by out-training the US but via: (a) algorithmic/architectural efficiency (distillation, MoE, smaller-model wins), (b) open-weight proliferation to maximize reach per scarce FLOP, (c) inference on abundant mature-node domestic silicon + aggressive power build-out (where it has no gate), and (d) throwing more chips (quantity over quality) at the problem, absorbing yield loss via state subsidy. It becomes a credible-but-capped second pole — exactly the GV27 ceiling. The US/allies retain the frontier-training cost advantage as long as EUV+High-NA+EDA hold, so US export-control policy is effectively AI-capability policy. The EU (Netherlands/Germany) holds the single most valuable veto in the graph and can extract concessions from BOTH sides — but faces rising incentive to defect as its China market shrinks. Net: a bifurcated compute world — a high-efficiency frontier (US-allied) and a subsidized, efficiency-forced, mature-node-heavy pole (China).

**…if it BREAKS:** If the gate BREAKS (China gets yield-viable indigenous EUV before ~2028, or allied controls fracture), the strategic picture inverts fast: China gains a domestic path to sub-3nm, and because it already leads on power generation, mature-node capacity, packaging scale and state capital, the US loses its principal structural lever over Chinese frontier AI — controls become a spent one-time delay rather than a durable moat (the COCOM endgame). Frontier training silicon would no longer be a US-allied near-monopoly input; China could pursue brute-force scaling AND efficiency simultaneously, collapsing the second-pole ceiling (GV27) and removing the main reason China stays open-weight/efficiency-first. This is the single highest-leverage counterfactual in the whole race — a forecaster should price a ~15-25% chance of a meaningful crack by 2030 (LDP maturing faster than expected OR EU/Japan defection), and treat any credible >200W domestic EUV source at production yield as the key early-warning trigger to re-rate the entire China-AI trajectory.

**Why it's soft_slow:** Not a physical impossibility but a slow-clearing execution tax with three stacked bottlenecks operating on different clocks: (1) interconnection queue/study process — 55+ month median, softened only by FERC reform (Order 2023) and behind-the-meter siting that bypasses the queue entirely; (2) equipment — large power transformers at 28-40 month lead times, gated by a single US grain-oriented-electrical-steel producer and a global oligopoly of ~3-4 HV transformer makers with tacit process knowledge and multi-year backlogs (this is the near-hard, GV8-controlled sub-gate); (3) HV transmission — the hardest, gated by multi-state permitting, right-of-way/NIMBY, and cost-allocation disputes that make new interregional lines take 10-15 years. Money accelerates (2) and partially (1) but barely touches (3). China faces none of these because vertically-integrated state ownership collapses permitting, financing, and equipment supply into a single directed actor.

**Why the trend moved this way:** The US gate tightened because a demand shock (AI datacenter load doubling every ~3-4 years, ~90 GW of new peak load by 2030) hit a supply system that had been slowing for a decade. Transmission construction fell from ~1,700 mi/yr (2010-14) to ~350 mi/yr (2020-23) as the easy renewable-integration lines got built and interregional projects drowned in multi-state permitting and cost-allocation fights. Simultaneously the queue quadrupled (500->2,600 GW, 2015-2023) as cheap solar+storage requests piled up faster than study processes — designed for a slow-growth era — could clear them, pushing median waits from 3 to 5+ years. The transformer/equipment layer then compounded it: post-2021 global demand surged (GSU demand +274% since 2019) against a supply base with one US GOES supplier and years-long backlogs, tripling lead times and raising prices 77%. Crucially, the US CAN add generation nameplate fast (48.6 GW in 2024, ~63 GW in 2025 — mostly solar+storage) — the bind is not making electrons, it's connecting and MOVING them. China, by contrast, added 429 GW (2024) and ~540 GW (2025) with a $500B+/yr state-directed build and controls the transformer/grid-equipment supply chain the US must import from — so the same physical task is a solved problem there and a multi-year tax here.

Highest-variance, lowest-probability-of-fast-fix lever.

**Historical analogies:**

Simultaneously the diffusion engine runs fast: each capability level commoditizes to open weights in ~4-6 months and collapses ~10x/yr in cost. So the gate is a RACE between two exponentials — capability-toward-threshold vs diffusion-equalizing-the-prior-level — and the race is genuinely undecided because the threshold-defining variable (RSI-depth) is the one thing not moving.

**Driving factors (past → future):**

**Why it's policy_reversible:** Two-layer gate. The TRIGGER (Beijing's decision to invade/blockade) is a policy switch — reversible, low base-rate, extreme magnitude, and can flip fast on a strength-window/diversionary/succession shock. But it sits on a HARD economic-physical floor: Mutual-Assured-Production-Destruction. A kinetic or blockade event zeroes ~90% of the world's leading-edge logic — including the chips China itself needs for its AI trajectory — while degrading the US military that would respond. TSMC cannot be captured functional: the fabs run on ASML EUV (GV2, embargoed to China), a continuous supply of Dutch/US/Japanese spares and process chemicals, and ~thousands of tacit-knowledge engineers (GV12) who do not transfer with the building. That hard floor is what makes the soft trigger unlikely — deterrence is 'holding' precisely because the calculus is dominated by MAPD, not because coercion is falling (it is rising sharply).

**Why the trend moved this way:** Two trends move in opposite directions and their crossing defines the gate. Net: the silicon shield is getting THICKER (more valuable to preserve, more catastrophic to break) at the same time the military threat sharpens. Deterrence holds not because tension is easing but because MAPD deepens — and because China's own frontier-chip fallback (self-sufficiency only ~33%, no EUV) means it would be the biggest loser of a TSMC blackout.

**Driving factors (past → future):**

**Why it's soft_slow:** Two-layer gate. (1) The WEAPON is policy-reversible and fast: export licensing can be turned on (Aug-2023 gallium, Apr-2025 magnets) and off (Jun-2025 trade deal) in weeks — past embargoes (2010 Japan) lasted ~2 months. (2) The MOAT behind it is soft-but-durable: rebuilding it is a 10-15yr slow tax, not a physical impossibility. The durability is not physics — separation chemistry is public — but (a) toxic-waste/permitting: solvent-extraction cascades produce radioactive thorium tailings needing decade-long environmental builds in the West; (b) tacit process knowledge in tier-3 sintered high-coercivity magnet fabrication (the true bottleneck); (c) China's ability to flood the market and destroy the economics of any nascent Western processor (the classic price-war moat). So: fast switch, slow-to-neutralize moat.

**Why the trend moved this way:** Why China dominates and why it's stuck-high: China didn't win on ore (mining share actually FELL from ~97% to ~58-69% as Myanmar/US/Australia scaled). It won by owning the midstream — separation, metallization, and above all sintered high-performance magnet fabrication — built over 30 years via cheap capital, lax environmental rules that let it absorb the toxic thorium-tailings burden the West offshored, and deliberate below-cost pricing that repeatedly bankrupted Western processors (Molycorp went bankrupt in 2015). The result is a dashboard that diverges: mining diversifies (metric 2 closing) while processing and magnets stay pinned at ~90-99% (metrics 1, 5 stuck/widening). China then converted that structural moat into an active weapon: gallium/germanium licensing (2023), a formal US ban (Dec-2024), and the April-2025 'one batch, one licence' magnet regime that within weeks forced auto/robotics plants in the US, EU, Japan and India to cut output or idle lines. The weapon is reversible (metric 7 rebounded after the June-2025 deal) — but the moat that makes it credible is not quickly erodible, because the West's counter-lever (metric 6, MP Materials) is doubling yearly yet still ~2 orders of magnitude below Chinese separated-NdPr volume, and Western magnet capacity doesn't arrive until ~2028.

**Driving factors (past → future):**
- **Midstream processing + tier-3 magnet capacity (not ore)** — *past:* China's edge came from separation/metallization/sintered-magnet fab, absorbing toxic tailings the West wouldn't. Mining share fell to ~58-69% while processing stayed ~91% and magnets ~94% — proof the moat lives midstream. *→ future:* The next leg is HARDER for the West at exactly the tier-3 magnet step. Even fully diversified mining + separation dead-ends without high-coercivity sintered-magnet lines, which need tacit process knowledge and arrive at Western scale only ~2028+. This is the near-hard gate behind the soft one.
- **Environmental/permitting + capital economics (the price-war moat)** — *past:* Western processors (Molycorp) died on cost/permitting while China priced below cost. Radioactive thorium tailings made Western separation slow and unpopular. *→ future:* Diversification is a 10-15yr toxic-waste build, and any nascent Western processor is exposed to a Chinese price flood the moment prices normalize. Requires guaranteed offtake/price floors (DoD/MP-style deals) or it stalls. This is why it's a slow tax, not a knockout.
- **Export-licensing statecraft (the reversible switch)** — *past:* 2010 Japan embargo, 2023 gallium/germanium, Dec-2024 US ban, Apr-2025 magnet licensing — each a policy switch, not a supply change. Prices (gallium 7-9x) and export volumes (magnets -75% then +157%) moved on paperwork. *→ future:* China will keep rationing via licensing to extract concessions (mirror of US chip controls) rather than a total cutoff, which would accelerate substitution. Expect volatile, calibrated squeezes — the lever is used to TAX and DETER, not to permanently deny.
- **MAD interdependence with US chip chokepoint (GV4)** — *past:* The Jun-2025 rebound came from a bilateral deal explicitly linking rare earths to broader tech trade — each side's chokepoint deterred the other's full use. *→ future:* Full weaponization is self-deterring: a total cutoff would both crater China's own magnet export revenue and hand the West the political will to fund 10-15yr substitution. Keeps the lever mostly holstered — a standoff, not a shooting war.

**Historical analogies:**
- **China 2010 rare-earth embargo on Japan (Senkaku crisis)** (web_verified): China halted REE exports to Japan (then ~90% dependent) for ~2 months; prices spiked ~10x through 2011. Japan responded with stockpiling, Lynas/Australia offtake, recycling and substitution R&D, and cut its China dependence from ~90% to ~58% over a decade. — *implies here:* The single best template: the WEAPON is short and reversible (months), but it durably motivates diversification of the diversifiable layers (mining, stockpiles) while the processing/magnet dependence erodes only slowly and partially. Predicts today's US path — real progress on ore/stockpiles, sticky dependence on separation + magnets. Breaks in that today's controls are surgical licensing (calibrated, ongoing) rather than a blunt 2-month halt, so the price signal is more sustained.
- **WWII US synthetic-rubber crash program (Rubber Reserve Co., 1942-45)** (corpus): Japan cut ~90% of natural rubber supply; a $700M state-coordinated program across 51 plants and 5 firms achieved near-full substitution in ~3 years. — *implies here:* Sets the FLOOR on how fast denial can be beaten — but only under war-economy mobilization with no permitting/environmental/cost friction and known substitution chemistry. Implies the US COULD compress the 10-15yr timeline IF it treats GV8 as a wartime priority (DoD offtake, price floors, permitting waivers — the MP Materials deal is an embryonic version). Breaks because rare-earth separation + sintered-magnet fab is far more complex than SBR chemistry and today's peacetime environmental review reintroduces exactly the friction 1942 suspended.
- **BASF Haber-Bosch synthetic nitrogen monopoly (1913-1930s)** (corpus): Germany's near-monopoly on synthetic ammonia persisted ~10-15 years after the process was publicly disclosed, because operational/tacit knowledge — not the recipe — was the moat. — *implies here:* Predicts the magnet/processing gap persists a decade-plus even though separation chemistry is public — capability transfer needs tacit process mastery and yield learning, not IP. Directly explains why metric 5 (magnets) stays ~90%+ despite Western intent. Breaks in that rare-earth processing is less of a single high-pressure step and more re-buildable with enough capital + guaranteed demand than a WWI-era monopoly, so the West's floor is faster than Germany's was.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERATE Western neutralization: (1) DoD/price-floor offtake deals de-risk processors against Chinese price floods (MP Materials 2025 deal is the template); (2) magnet recycling + reduced-heavy-REE motor designs (Tesla, Niron iron-nitride) cut demand for the exact grades China controls; (3) a hard Chinese overreach (total cutoff) that hands the West wartime political will à la 1942. STALL/REVERSE Western progress: (1) China floods the market and normalizes prices the moment Western capex commits, bankrupting nascent processors (the Molycorp 2015 replay); (2) permitting/thorium-tailings litigation drags US/EU separation builds past a decade; (3) tier-3 sintered-magnet know-how proves stickier than expected, so even ex-China separated oxide still routes through Chinese magnet makers; (4) the MAD linkage with chips holds, so neither side pushes substitution to completion and dependence ossifies.

**Forward call:** By 2028: China still controls ~80-88% of REE processing and ~85%+ of high-performance magnets; ex-China processing reaches only ~15-20% (matching the 'partially diversified' state), driven almost entirely by MP Materials, Lynas, and allied builds, with the first at-scale US magnet plant (MP '10X') just coming online. Gallium/germanium remain ~95%+ Chinese with no material Western substitute. Transformer lead times ease modestly (~90-110 weeks) as $600M+ of announced US capacity lands, but China-sourced grid inputs persist. ~75% probability GV8 stays 'China-dominant, weaponizable' through 2028. By 2030: magnets/processing diversify to ~20-25% ex-China (durable but incomplete), gallium remains a hard chokepoint, and the lever is used as a calibrated tax/deterrent rather than a full embargo. ~60% probability it is still China's most usable non-chip counter-lever in 2030; ~25% it is meaningfully neutralized for magnets specifically (via recycling + redesign + allied capacity); <10% it is fully substituted. Net: a soft, durable lever that taxes the US buildout +15-25% and +6-12 months — a tax, not a knockout — and one China keeps mostly holstered because of MAD with chips.

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If GV8 holds, the US AI buildout is taxed at its FIRST physical step, not its compute step — a materially different constraint than the chip race. Concretely and second-order: (US) Data-center power provisioning stays gated by transformer/grid-equipment lead times (~2yr+) with GOES-steel, gallium and magnet inputs sitting behind a Chinese chokepoint → the binding constraint on US AI scaling migrates from GPUs to megawatts-delivered, raising $/MW and pushing capex +15-25% and timelines +6-12mo on the power layer. Expect the US response to be (a) DoD-style guaranteed offtake to stand up domestic processing (MP model), (b) friend-shoring processing to Australia/allies (an 'IEA-for-minerals' MSP), and (c) demand-side redesign — heavy-REE-free motors, gallium-free power electronics, aggressive magnet recycling — rather than out-refining China. Crucially, because this is a power/infrastructure tax and not a compute tax, it slows the PACE and raises the COST of US inference/training capacity buildout but does not cap frontier model capability the way an EUV denial caps China. (China) Gains a calibrated deterrent that mirrors US chip controls — usable to extract trade concessions (as in Jun-2025) and to deter maximal US chip pressure, but self-limiting: over-use accelerates the substitution that erodes the moat. (Allies) Australia/Canada/EU become the swing processors; whoever funds the toxic-waste midstream first captures durable leverage. Net strategic read: the AI race is decided less by who can weaponize this and more by whether the US converts a soft-slow tax into a managed cost — most likely YES over 5-10yr, so GV8 is a friction and bargaining chip, not a decisive constraint on US AI outcomes.

**…if it BREAKS:** Two break directions. (1) Gate CLEARS faster than expected (Western processing + magnets hit ~30%+ ex-China by 2028 via war-footing offtake + recycling + redesign): China loses its principal non-chip counter-lever, the MAD balance with GV4 tilts toward the US, and Beijing's incentive to escalate chip retaliation rises precisely because its deterrent is decaying — a more dangerous, less stable standoff even as the US buildout de-risks. US power-layer capex tax falls back toward baseline; transformers/magnets stop being the binding constraint. (2) China WEAPONIZES fully (total, sustained cutoff, not calibrated licensing): near-term shock — US/allied EV, wind, robotics, defense and grid-equipment lines idle, power buildout timelines blow out well past +12mo, gallium-dependent defense electronics hit hard. But historically (2010 Japan, 1942 rubber) a blunt cutoff triggers a mobilized crash program that neutralizes the moat in 3-7yr instead of 10-15 — so a full break would hurt acutely then durably destroy China's own leverage and export revenue. A forecaster should price the calibrated-tax path as most likely, the fast-clear as the medium-term drift, and the full-weaponization as a low-probability, high-severity tail that is self-defeating for China.

The exceptions (embedded workflow + proprietary data + reorg) are still nascent. *→ future:* This is the swing factor for 2028-2030. If workflow apps build Bloomberg/Oracle-style moats (proprietary data + retraining cost + org lock-in, 10-15yr durability), rent lands there and the smile-curve resolves toward the application end. If not, rent stays trapped at the physical layer and the app layer stays a low-margin commodity. The GV31 data-licensing ratchet grandfathering incumbent corpora tilts this toward whoever already holds the data.

**Historical analogies:**
- **Linux/Android/cloud open-base pattern — commoditized software, proprietary rent captured at the hosting/distribution layer** (corpus): Open-source or commoditized base layers (Linux, Android, open web stacks) did not destroy rent — they relocated it to the firms that aggregated, hosted, and distributed on top (AWS/GCP/Azure monetized open-source; Google monetized Android via Play + services). The reference-architecture builder rarely kept the rent. — *implies here:* Predicts precisely today's migration: the commoditizing model layer (open weights) will NOT be the rent locus; rent lands at the cloud/aggregation + hardware layers that host it. Breaks where AI adds a NEW scarce input the open-source era lacked — physical compute + power scarcity — which concentrates rent even harder at the hardware layer than the software-only precedent did.
- **CUDA vs. CANN / RISC-V-style geopolitical escape valve (open ISA vs. proprietary ARM)** (corpus): RISC-V shows open standards commoditize the MIDDLE of the performance distribution but lag at the frontier; states adopt them as national-security escape valves from proprietary lock-in without displacing the incumbent globally. TCP/IP-vs-OSI shows global de facto standards resist government-mandate displacement but the mandating jurisdiction gets a real domestic alternative (bifurcation, not global replacement). — *implies here:* Predicts NVIDIA/CUDA rent stays globally dominant but China builds a genuine CANN-based domestic rent pool inside its walled garden — a bifurcated locus, not a toppled one. Breaks because AI hardware switching costs (engineer-years of kernel rewrites + model revalidation) are far higher than TCP/IP's recompile-the-stack, so the incumbent moat is MORE durable than the OSI precedent — China gets real domestic CANN training runs but no global displacement.
- **Bloomberg terminal / Oracle workflow-and-ecosystem moat (10-15yr durability) vs. Hadoop/Cloudera commoditization dead-end** (corpus): Bloomberg (~$12B/yr, ~350k seats, 40yr unbroken) and Oracle held rent for decades on switching costs + workflow embedding + proprietary data, resisting well-funded challengers. Conversely, being the enterprise distributor of an open-source base (Cloudera/Hortonworks on Hadoop) was NOT a durable moat and collapsed. — *implies here:* These bracket the application-layer outcome. IF AI apps build proprietary-data + workflow + reorg lock-in, they earn Bloomberg-grade durability and rent lands at the app layer. IF they are thin wrappers on open models, they are Cloudera and capture nothing. Breaks because Bloomberg faced no state-backed adversary and no export-control regime, whereas AI's locus is being actively shaped by industrial policy — a variable the pure-commercial precedents omit.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERANTS of the current locus (rent stays concentrated at hardware+cloud): continued frontier capability gains that keep training compute-hungry; inference demand exploding faster than efficiency (so total GPU/cloud spend keeps compounding); EU AI Act and similar rules entrenching the compliance-bundle; capex intensity staying at a level only 3-5 firms can fund. STALLS / REVERSALS (rent disperses or migrates): (1) NVIDIA's ~73% margin cracking as custom silicon (TPU/Trainium/MTIA) captures in-house inference and AMD/China erode share — the first quantitative tell; (2) algorithmic efficiency (distillation, smaller models, on-device) collapsing the compute bill so the hardware pool shrinks relative to value; (3) antitrust/mandated-access remedies (GV33) prying open the compute+compliance bundle; (4) EU AI Act rollback (GV30) deflating the compliance-moat premise; (5) a genuine application-layer moat forming (Bloomberg-style) that pulls rent up-stack to workflow owners; (6) sovereign bifurcation (China CANN bloc + Gulf/EU sovereign clouds) fracturing the single global rent pool.

**Forward call:** By 2028: rent stays concentrated at the hardware + hyperscaler-cloud layers, with NVIDIA still the largest single rent-capturer but margin beginning to compress (call: NVIDIA data-center gross margin down from ~73% toward ~65-68% as custom silicon captures a rising share of INFERENCE; ~70% probability). The pure model layer is decisively NOT a durable rent locus (~85% probability) — labs monetize only via cloud-parent bundling or app integration. By 2030: the locus splits into (a) a still-dominant US hardware+cloud rent pool (NVIDIA + big-three, share of value high but margins normalized), (b) a genuinely bifurcated China CANN/domestic-silicon rent pool inside the walled garden (~65% probability this is real and non-trivial by 2030), and (c) a partially-formed application/workflow rent layer where Bloomberg-style winners emerge in data-rich verticals (finance, healthcare, legal) but most apps stay thin-margin (~50/50 whether the app layer captures 'durable' rent by 2030). Net committed call: the physical/aggregation layers hold the majority of durable AI rent through 2030; the smile-curve means the US captures the physical + aggregation ends while the reorg/app end is contested and geographically dispersed. The Gulf remains a landlord, not an owner (>80% probability), capturing land/power/hosting margin but not GV1/GV2/GV5 model or IP rent.

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If rent stays locked at hardware+cloud (the base case): (1) CAPABILITY — frontier AI capability keeps requiring routing money through NVIDIA + the big-three, so the firms that set the pace of AI are infrastructure owners, not model labs; model quality becomes near-free at the median and differentiation moves to scale of deployed compute and proprietary data/workflow. (2) COST — inference cost per token keeps falling (~10x/12-18mo) but the AGGREGATE bill rises because volume outruns unit-price decline (Jevons), so the rent pool compounds even as models commoditize — good for AI diffusion, but value accrues to whoever owns the meter (clouds) and the meter's chips (NVIDIA). (3) PER-ACTOR — US: wins decisively; hyperscalers convert regulation into a compliance-bundle moat and own compute+distribution, so US captures the durable rent even though the model layer (its most visible asset) commoditizes. China: capped out of the US hardware rent pool, so it competes exactly as GV10 predicts — efficiency + open weights + inference on mature/domestic nodes + a walled-garden CANN rent pool it fully controls; it does not out-train the US but it denies NVIDIA the Chinese rent and builds sovereign capacity. Gulf: structurally a landlord — captures power/land/hosting spread but not model/IP rent, so its AI 'ownership' is shallow and reversible. Labs (OpenAI/Anthropic): survive only by integrating up (agents/apps) or being the compute-demand subsidiary of a cloud parent — pure model-layer independence is not a durable rent position. (4) SECOND-ORDER — power and firm-power interconnection become the true upstream chokepoint on the hardware rent (feeding back to power gates), and antitrust becomes the only lever that could redistribute rent, but on too slow a clock to matter before 2028.

**…if it BREAKS:** Counterfactual the forecaster must price: (A) if algorithmic efficiency + on-device inference collapse the compute bill (models get radically cheaper to run), the hardware rent pool SHRINKS relative to value, NVIDIA's ~73% margin cracks hard, and rent migrates UP to whoever owns the user relationship and proprietary data — the application/workflow layer wins (Bloomberg outcome), which favors incumbents with distribution (Microsoft/Google via Copilot/Workspace, and vertical-SaaS with proprietary data) over pure-infra players. (B) if a genuine frontier capability moat reopens (a model tier open weights simply cannot reach — reliable long-horizon agents), model-layer rent RE-CONCENTRATES at 2-3 labs and the 'model layer is commoditized' thesis reverses — labs become the rent locus and clouds/hardware revert to suppliers. (C) if antitrust/mandated-access (GV33) actually pries open the compute+compliance bundle before 2028, the aggregation moat fragments and rent disperses toward a more competitive cloud market + neoclouds. (D) if sovereign bifurcation runs faster than expected (EU + Gulf + China all build protected domestic rent pools), there is NO single global rent-capture locus — the 'sovereign-bifurcated' state wins, and value is captured nationally behind policy walls rather than by a global oligopoly. The highest-impact break to watch is (A): the efficiency-driven collapse of the hardware rent pool, because it is the only one that moves on a fast (soft) clock rather than a slow policy clock.

**Why it's soft_slow:** Two coupled mechanisms with different hardness. (1) The PIPELINE is soft-but-slow and structurally inelastic: a frontier researcher is ~5-7 years of PhD-plus-postdoc human-capital formation that cannot be bought forward with money — you cannot compress the clock, only start it earlier. That makes the STOCK of elite talent slow to build and slow to erode. (2) The CONCENTRATION is soft and fast-eroding in principle (knowledge walks across borders — the sharpest historical control-failure mode) but is held in place by ECOSYSTEM GRAVITY: co-located peers, frontier compute, capital, and open-publication norms that make the marginal value of any single defector low. (3) The US self-inflicted portion (H-1B caps, green-card backlog) is purely policy_reversible — a switch, not a wall. Net: the gate is soft_slow because the binding constraint is time-to-form-humans and the stickiness of a dense cluster, not a physical impossibility; but its US-disadvantaging component could flip fast with immigration reform.

**Why the trend moved this way:** The paradox the data resolves: China's PRODUCTION of elite talent exploded (undergrad-in-China share of top researchers 27%→38%; top-100-cited papers ~20→41) yet the US WORK-location share barely moved (51-59% band, 2017-2025). Why? Ecosystem gravity. The US didn't out-produce talent — it IMPORTED it (72% of China-educated elite work in the US) because the frontier labs, the largest compute, the deepest capital, and the densest peer clusters are all co-located there, and comp escalated to $100-200M to nail the power-law core in place. The pipeline is inelastic, so neither side can sprint; the US led by winning the location game, not the birth-rate game. What is now shifting is the RETENTION derivative: Chinese-PhD stay rates fell 90%→76%, and DeepSeek proved a frontier-grade model can be built by a team trained ENTIRELY in China — the first evidence the 'nowhere to go' problem (that doomed Russian reconstitution and India's IIT diaspora) no longer applies to China. That path's ceiling: China can indigenize a self-sufficient frontier talent base, but it cannot easily strip the US of the elite already embedded in US labs — diffusion and parallel build-up, not wholesale reversal.

**Driving factors (past → future):**
- **Ecosystem gravity (co-located compute + capital + peers + open-publication norms)** — *past:* Held US work-share flat at ~55-59% even as China became the #1 source; the US won by being the place elite talent chose to WORK, not the place it was born. Open-publication culture (arXiv/NeurIPS) simultaneously LOWERED the strategic value of any single defector — the anti-Fuchs dynamic. *→ future:* As long as the largest training clusters, capital, and frontier peers stay in the US, imported talent keeps landing there — the container holds. It erodes only if a rival cluster (Hangzhou/Beijing labs, or a Gulf-funded hub) reaches peer density AND frontier compute. DeepSeek is the first crack; watch whether it becomes a self-reinforcing cluster.
- **US immigration policy (H-1B cap, green-card backlog) — the self-inflicted switch** — *past:* Selection odds collapsed 46%→25% (2021-24); India EB backlog >1M with 15-18yr waits. This taxed the front door and made staying a decade-plus limbo, nudging marginal talent toward EU/Canada/home — the only lever the US turns against itself. *→ future:* Policy_reversible in BOTH directions. FY2027 wage-weighted selection + beneficiary-centric reform already lifted odds to 35% and tilts toward high-skill — if paired with green-card relief or a startup/genius visa, the US could widen its lead cheaply. If instead restriction tightens (broad visa crackdowns), it hands China and the EU a recruiting gift precisely as their domestic options improve.
- **China's domestic absorptive capacity (frontier labs + commercial demand)** — *past:* The decisive variable that separates a successful Taiwan-style repatriation from a failed Russian-style one. China's Alibaba/ByteDance/DeepSeek ecosystem gave returnees somewhere to go; DeepSeek showed >50% domestically-trained teams can hit the frontier, and elite stay-in-US rates began falling. *→ future:* If domestic labs keep producing frontier-grade work and pay competitively, retention rises and the pipeline surge finally converts into a home-based elite — indigenization. This is the factor most likely to move the gate's state 2028→2030. Its ceiling is compute access (gated by export controls, GV1) and whether a few marquee labs deepen into a true cluster.
- **Comp escalation & the price of the power-law core** — *past:* Peak packages went ~$2M→$200M in a decade, proving talent is the marginal binding input and that US private capital will pay almost anything to retain the few hundred who matter. *→ future:* Sustains US retention of the embedded elite even under bad visa policy — money substitutes for policy at the very top. But it does nothing for the early-career pipeline gated by H-1B, and it prices EU/Gulf out of the top tier entirely (their comp/tax structures cannot match), locking in their binding constraint.

**Historical analogies:**
- **Taiwan 'sea turtle' reverse migration → ITRI → Hsinchu → TSMC (1973-2005)** (corpus): State-directed diaspora repatriation: ITRI recruited US-trained Taiwanese engineers with stipends, housing, IP-sharing and equity, coupled to state-built industrial infrastructure (Hsinchu Science Park), compounding over 30 years into TSMC's world leadership. — *implies here:* The most direct proof-of-concept for China's strategy: repatriation WORKS when domestic industrial infrastructure exists to absorb returnees. DeepSeek is China's early 'sea turtle' signal. Where it breaks: China's AI diaspora is more globally dispersed and heterogeneous than Taiwan's dense Stanford/Berkeley clusters (weaker peer-pressure-to-return), and AI's compute co-factor is export-gated in a way Taiwan's tooling was not — so the compounding may be slower and compute-capped.
- **Post-Soviet Russian brain drain & failed reconstitution via Skolkovo/megagrants (1991-2015)** (corpus): 500-800k scientists emigrated (30-40% of the workforce); later state programs (Skolkovo, megagrants) failed to reconstitute a frontier base because there was 'nowhere to go' — no commercial-industrial absorptive capacity and no translation from theory to product. — *implies here:* The counterfactual that proves money+will are necessary but not sufficient — the decisive variable is domestic absorption. It predicts China does NOT automatically win the return game. Where it breaks: China's collapse-free, commercially vibrant ecosystem (Alibaba/ByteDance/DeepSeek) is the opposite of Russia's — the 'nowhere to go' problem that doomed Russia largely does not bind China, which is why China is more Taiwan than Russia.
- **Klaus Fuchs / Manhattan Project human-vector knowledge transfer (1942-1949)** (corpus): A handful of embedded physicists transferred bottlenecked, classified design knowledge (implosion geometry) that compressed the Soviet timeline by 2-3 years. — *implies here:* Defines WHEN human-vector transfer is decisive: tacit, unpublished, bottlenecked knowledge held by few. It predicts the OPPOSITE for AI: the strong open-publication culture (arXiv/NeurIPS) and distributed nature of AI capability mean no single 'Fuchs' can compress timelines — the marginal defector matters far less. This is why the US work-share can stay high even as talent is globally sourced: the knowledge is already half-public, so location of bodies matters more than secrecy.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERATE US lead: FY2027 wage-weighted H-1B + a startup/'genius' visa + green-card backlog relief would convert the world's largest imported pipeline into a durable moat cheaply; sustained comp dominance keeps the embedded elite locked in. ACCELERATE diffusion to China: continued fall in Chinese stay rates (76%→below 70%), more DeepSeek-class domestic labs reaching the frontier, and a US visa/security crackdown (expanded 'China Initiative'-style scrutiny) that pushes ethnic-Chinese researchers out — this is the fastest reversal path and it is US-triggered. STALL/REVERSE China: export-control-driven compute scarcity (GV1) starving domestic labs so returnees again have 'nowhere to go at the frontier,' reproducing the Russian failure mode. EU/Gulf wildcards: an EU capital-markets-union + equity-comp/tax fix could staunch its founder drain (low probability); Gulf sovereign capital could buy a hub but cannot buy a research community fast.

**Forward call:** 2028: The container still holds. US hosts ~55-60% of elite AI researchers by work location (P≈0.70) — ecosystem gravity + $100M+ comp beat visa friction for the embedded core. But the retention derivative has turned: Chinese-PhD stay rates keep sliding and 2-4 more DeepSeek-class domestically-trained labs reach the frontier, so the state reads 'hyper-concentrated but diffusing (leaky container).' EU stays BINDING-drained; Gulf stays import-dependent. 2030: Bifurcation, not reversal. The US retains the single deepest cluster, but China has indigenized a self-sufficient frontier talent base that no longer depends on US-return (P≈0.60 China reaches 'returning/indigenizing') — parallel poles, with the gap set by compute access (GV1), not talent supply. Probability the US LOSES its work-location plurality by 2030: low (~15%), and if it happens it will be self-inflicted via a visa/security crackdown, not out-competed.

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If the trend holds (US concentration persists but leaks; China indigenizes in parallel): (1) US — keeps the ability to execute the most frontier training runs and efficiency breakthroughs (gates GV10) because the power-law core stays put; its edge is IMPORTED and therefore contingent on not self-sabotaging. Strategically it should treat immigration policy as an AI-industrial weapon (wage-weighted visas, green-card relief) — cheaper than a fab. (2) China — no longer needs to out-import; it competes at the frontier via a homegrown cohort (DeepSeek path) plus efficiency + open weights + inference on mature nodes, and crucially its returnee/domestic talent can supply the tacit process knowledge that makes a seized/indigenized fab (GV1) or captured lab (GV34) FUNCTIONAL rather than a shell — talent partially substitutes for both indigenization and kinetic options (GV4). (3) EU — the talent wedge, not regulation, is its dominant capability gap; without a comp/tax/capital fix it remains a training ground that exports its best founders to the US, capping it as a fast-follower. (4) Gulf — capital and energy buy data centers but not a research community; it stays a compute-landlord/inference host reliant on revocable expat visas, structurally unable to reach the frontier tier. Second-order: because AI knowledge is semi-open (anti-Fuchs), leadership is decided by cluster density and compute, so the talent gate mostly AMPLIFIES the compute gate rather than acting independently.

**…if it BREAKS:** Counterfactual to price: If US concentration collapses faster than expected — most plausibly a self-inflicted visa/security crackdown that pushes ethnic-Chinese and other foreign-born researchers out during a period when China's domestic labs are hiring at the frontier — the US would lose the imported core that IS its lead (72% of China-educated elite currently work there). That would compress China's frontier timeline by years (the modern, distributed, non-Fuchs version of the atomic-espionage acceleration), hand China both talent AND the tacit knowledge to operationalize indigenized fabs/labs, and leave the US relying on compute and capital advantages alone. Conversely, if China's break is on the OTHER side — compute starvation (export controls) reproduces the Russian 'nowhere to go at the frontier' failure and its production surge never converts to retention — then China stays a talent EXPORTER, the US lead widens, and the gate ceases to be China's lever. The forecaster must watch the Chinese stay-rate and DeepSeek-cluster-count as the tell for which break is happening.

**Why it's soft_slow:** Composite gate with a hard core and soft edges. The HARD element is physical: frontier training needs leading-edge silicon (sub-7nm, HBM, CoWoS advanced packaging), gated upstream by GV3 (EUV/ASML) — a genuine chokepoint BIS licenses can switch on/off. But the export-control REGIME layered on top is soft: (a) it is a legal/administrative construct enforced at borders, defeated by transshipment through third countries (Epoch central ~660k H100e smuggled through 2025) and by static numeric thresholds that China-compliant SKU design (A800/H800/H20) walked around until each re-tightening; (b) the service/cloud-API leg is structurally uncontrollable by a goods regime — compute rented as a service crosses no customs border (the VoIP failure mode); ByteDance/Alibaba trained models via SE-Asian clouds, and the US only began closing it via the Remote Access Security Act in 2025-26; (c) coalition cohesion depends on three governments (US/NL/Japan) whose firms (NVIDIA, ASML, Tokyo Electron) are the paying losers — ASML China sales hit 41% in 2024, NVIDIA wrote down ~$4.5B on H20 — so commercial lobbying + defection erode it; (d) it is reversible by executive action (May 2025 AI Diffusion Rule rescission, Gulf deals). Net: a slow cost/time TAX on China's frontier compute (delay, not denial) — unless upgraded with API/attestation/compute-governance controls (the CWC/OPCW template).

**Why the trend moved this way:** The regime is genuinely tightening on the ADMINISTRATIVE axis (Entity List ~5.5x since 2016 to ~1,300; FDPR extraterritoriality; Jan 2023 trilateral equipment regime; Dec 2 2024 HBM+SME+~140-entity package) because the US learned that static single-parameter thresholds get gamed: NVIDIA legally shipped A800/H800 by dialing interconnect just under the Oct 2022 dual test (TPP 4,800 AND 600 GB/s), so BIS dropped the 600 GB/s interconnect parameter and added a performance-density metric in Oct 2023 (TPP 4,800 OR TPP 1,600 + PD 5.92), then moved to component-level HBM controls (3A090.c) in Dec 2024. Each move closes the last workaround. But three counter-currents keep it soft. (1) Commercial self-undermining: the enforcement agent is also the loser — NVIDIA's China share fell 26.4%→13.1% then 'to zero' post-H20 ban (~$4.5B charge), and it lobbied successfully for 2025 reversals; ASML China sales spiked to 41% in 2024 on stockpiling. (2) Circumvention: Singapore billing share hit 22%, ~660k H100e reportedly smuggled cumulatively (Epoch), and cloud-API rental sidesteps goods controls entirely. (3) Substitution: denial accelerates Huawei Ascend (roughly doubling yearly, HBM-capped) and efficiency (DeepSeek-V3 trained for a reported $5.6M final-run on H800s, ~60% of H100 achievable on Ascend 910C inference) that void the leverage premise. So the trend is 'tightening on paper, leaking in practice' — the ratchet closes hardware vectors fast, but the service leg and indigenization convert denial into delay.

**Driving factors (past → future):**
- **Commercial-loss lobbying vs. security hawks inside the US government (the self-undermining enforcement agent)** — *past:* NVIDIA designed compliant SKUs (A800/H800/H20) to keep selling into each new threshold and won marginal loosenings (H20 permitted 2024, AI Diffusion Rule rescinded May 2025) — keeping the regime porous even as hawks tightened lists; it booked a ~$4.5B H20 charge fighting to preserve ~20-25% of DC revenue. *→ future:* As NVIDIA's frontier margins increasingly depend on non-China demand (US/Gulf hyperscalers), the China revenue at stake shrinks relative to total — paradoxically making it EASIER for hawks to write China off and tighten, but the same firm pushes re-export volume to the Gulf/allies where it can still book revenue, widening the third-country leak.
- **The goods-vs-service boundary (cloud-API / open-weight leakage)** — *past:* Controls only ever bit on physical chip imports; ByteDance planned a ~$7B strategy to rent Nvidia compute via SE-Asian/Gulf clouds, Alibaba/ByteDance trained models offshore, and open-weight models (DeepSeek) diffused capability with no customs surface at all. *→ future:* This is the near-hard limit on the WHOLE regime: unless BIS extends to API/IaaS/attestation (the Remote Access Security Act, passed the House 2025-26, and KYC cloud rules), the service leg structurally defeats goods-controls. Whether the US crosses into internet-sovereignty-style controls is the single biggest fork for the gate's 2028-2030 efficacy.
- **Coalition cohesion (US–Netherlands–Japan) and third-country enforcement** — *past:* The Jan 2023 trilateral + Dutch DUV licensing (Sep 2023, expanded Sep 2024 to NXT:1970i/1980i) + Japan's 23-category rules (Jul 2023) extended the cordon; but China's stockpiling (ASML China share to 41% in 2024) and the 2025 suspension of the US Affiliates Rule under a trade truce show compliance lags intent. *→ future:* COCOM base rate: multilateral controls erode over 15-30yr under commercial pressure and defection. The next leg is HARDER to hold as ASML/TEL China revenue proves the cost and as more transshipment hubs (SE Asia, Gulf) must be policed — enforcement bandwidth, not law, becomes binding.
- **Speed of Chinese indigenization + algorithmic efficiency (delay converting to denial-defeat)** — *past:* Denial handed Huawei Ascend a captive market (910B ~200k units 2024, roughly doubling yearly) and forced efficiency gains (DeepSeek-V3 ~$5.6M final run on H800s); SMIC 7nm N+2 (DUV, ~30% yield) shipped in the Mate 60. *→ future:* If domestic HBM (CXMT) and SMIC 7nm yield scale, China's need for smuggled NVIDIA falls and the gate loses bite from the demand side; controls then only buy time. The ceiling is GV3 (EUV): sub-3nm stays gated and HBM is the binding constraint (CXMT ~2M stacks in 2026 caps Ascend at ~250-300k), so China stays ~1-2 nodes behind at the bleeding edge even as mid-tier need is met domestically.

**Historical analogies:**
- **COCOM → Wassenaar → BIS advanced-AI-chip controls (1949–present)** (corpus): Multilateral dual-use export controls on the Soviet bloc bought the West ~5-10 years of tech advantage but eroded under allied resentment, commercial friction, and the shift from a closed-bloc adversary to controls spanning 130+ states; Wassenaar's consensus structure made it slow and leaky. — *implies here:* Predicts the BIS regime buys ~5-10 years of Chinese frontier-training delay, then erodes as commercial pressure and defection accumulate — the base rate for the forward call. Breaks because software/weights/APIs cannot be inspected at a border, so digital leakage makes THIS regime erode faster than COCOM's hardware-only cordon.
- **VoIP/Skype circumventing PTT hardware-based telecom regulation (1995–2015)** (corpus): National telecom monopolies grounded in licensed physical hardware were categorically bypassed once capability was delivered as an internet service — regulators could not tax or license a software protocol riding commodity infrastructure. — *implies here:* Predicts the cloud-API/inference-as-a-service leg structurally defeats a goods-based chip regime: China accesses frontier capability (ByteDance/Alibaba offshore training) without importing a chip. Breaks because frontier TRAINING is compute-intensive ($100M+ data centers, natural GPU chokepoints) whereas VoIP ran on any commodity server — the analogy holds for capability ACCESS but the physical chokepoint for frontier training is real, making the gate soft on service, harder on training.
- **Commercial satellite imagery globalizing strategic reconnaissance (1999–present)** (corpus): US resolution restrictions on commercial imagery were progressively undercut as non-US providers and the market offered equivalent capability outside US jurisdiction, commoditizing a former state monopoly. — *implies here:* Predicts that once non-US compute providers (Gulf, indigenized China, allied clouds) offer equivalent AI capability outside US control, the chokepoint commoditizes and leverage decays. Breaks because AI training feedback loops compound capability asymmetries over time (unlike read-only imagery), so the leader's edge can persist longer than imagery's did — if the US keeps the frontier moving.

**Inflection risks:** ACCELERANTS (regime tightens/holds): BIS operationalizes cloud/API/IaaS KYC + the Remote Access Security Act (closes the VoIP leg — the single biggest possible upgrade); binding re-export enforcement on Singapore/Malaysia/UAE with revocation teeth; the '50% affiliates rule' (could expand reach ~15x); secondary sanctions on transshippers; allied lock-in of tool-servicing bans; a Taiwan/military trigger hardening consensus. STALLS/REVERSALS (regime erodes): a US administration trading controls in a broader deal (the 2025 Affiliates-Rule suspension is a live example); NVIDIA lobbying producing more H20-style reversals; DeepSeek-class efficiency + Huawei Ascend/CXMT-HBM scaling that makes controls pointless (voids the leverage premise, GV27); Gulf re-export becoming a de facto China pipeline; ASML/Japan defection as China revenue loss compounds; WTO/allied challenge to FDPR extraterritoriality.

**Forward call:** By 2028: the regime stays in a tightening/cohesive posture on hardware (Entity List keeps growing, FDPR holds, coalition intact) — ~70% probability it is still net-tightening on goods — BUT the effective denial on China's frontier compute degrades to a delay of ~1.5-2.5 years rather than a wall, because circumvention (~20-40% leak; Epoch ~1/3 of China compute smuggled) + Huawei Ascend scaling + DeepSeek-style efficiency erode the bite. ~55% chance the US has operationalized cloud/API controls in some form by 2028 (partial, contested). By 2030: the goods-control core persists but its strategic value decays toward the COCOM pattern — China self-sufficient at mid-tier (7nm-class) accelerators for domestic/inference needs, still ~1-2 nodes behind at the bleeding edge via the GV3/EUV + HBM gates, and the US leverage premise (GV27) substantially weakened by demonstrated Chinese efficiency-per-dollar parity. ~60% the regime reads as 'cohesive but circumvented' by 2030; ~25% 'eroding via deals/carve-outs'; ~15% 'tightened successfully' via compute-governance (CWC/OPCW-style attestation) that closes the service leg.

**Implications for AI outcomes — if the trend HOLDS:** If the regime HOLDS (stays tightening/cohesive and materially constrains China's leading-edge access): CHINA is capped for the largest frontier TRAINING runs — it cannot assemble H100/B200-class clusters at US/Gulf scale (HBM is the binding constraint, capping Ascend at ~250-300k/yr), so it competes asymmetrically: algorithmic efficiency (DeepSeek-style low-FLOP training, ~$5.6M final runs), open-weight release to commoditize the frontier and deny US labs pricing power, indigenized mid-tier silicon (Ascend at ~60% of H100 inference) for domestic inference, and talent extraction (GV12) to substitute tacit knowledge — i.e. it out-EFFICIENCIES rather than out-TRAINS. Net: China stays ~1-2 years behind at the absolute frontier but closes fast on deployed/applied AI and cheap inference. The US keeps a frontier-training lead and pricing power in premium AI and rents it to allies/Gulf as a diplomatic instrument — but its enforcement credibility is hostage to NVIDIA's revenue politics. The GULF becomes the swing region: frontier compute on revocable US permission (~500k GPUs/yr ceiling), so its AI-pole ambition is real but leashed — one policy reversal from being cut off — pushing it to hedge toward Chinese/indigenous supply. Second-order: a bifurcated compute world (US-stack frontier vs China-stack efficient/open) with the Gulf and Global South as the contested middle.

**…if it BREAKS:** If the regime BREAKS — via a grand-bargain deal, coalition defection (ASML/Japan), or the cloud-API leg proving structurally uncontrollable — export controls collapse into a mere cost tax that China routes around at ~20-40% leakage or higher. The US loses its primary non-kinetic lever over China's AI trajectory; frontier capability commoditizes faster (the satellite-imagery pattern), and DeepSeek-class parity voids the access-leverage premise entirely (GV27). NVIDIA recaptures China revenue, but the US forfeits strategic advantage: the race reverts to who has firm POWER + CAPITAL + TALENT (the energy/GV1 gates), not who controls chips — and China's scale advantages in power build-out and STEM throughput become decisive. A partial break (Gulf re-export becomes a de facto China pipeline) is the most likely failure mode: the US keeps the appearance of control while the third-country valve quietly equalizes access, meaning the forecaster should price coalition/enforcement erosion, not a dramatic single-day repeal.
