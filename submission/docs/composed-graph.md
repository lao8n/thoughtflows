# Composed cross-topic decision graph

**Worldview (read off the graph):** The composed graph is not a single race with one winner but a chokepoint lattice of actor-specific minimum staves running against two clocks. A SLOW physical clock governs the frontier: China is gated at leading-edge fabrication (GV1), itself gated behind the EUV/EDA tooling monopoly (GV2, where the EU quietly holds the tightest veto) and packaging (GV3); the US is gated not by capital or chips but by power/grid execution (GV6), which China taxes through its materials and grid-equipment refining (GV8) — so each great power holds a chokepoint on the other, a mutual-assured-DISRUPTION standoff (chips vs minerals/electrons) that neither can escape this decade, with Taiwan/TSMC (GV21) the one discontinuity that zeroes both sides at once. A FAST diffusion clock (GV10: efficiency + open-weight commoditization, amplified by talent mobility GV12 and circumvention GV4) simultaneously erodes every model-layer and export-control moat, pushing durable rent relentlessly up-stack (GV15) toward whoever owns power, compute, aggregation, or the reorganized application layer — often NOT the physical winner. The decisive meta-variable is GV11 (whether and when a decisive-capability threshold arrives versus the diffusion clock): pre-2032 transformative AI makes ~6-8 years of controls and lead-preservation coherent, while 2035+ lets diffusion, second-pole credibility (GV27), and Gulf/EU/swing-state poles (GV26/GV28) survive. Beneath all of it sits the monetary layer (GV36-GV42), which bifurcates by capital-account regime — the US faces visible open-market discipline where GV37/GV38 are binding, while China resolves the same fiscal pressure quietly — and which a Taiwan shock detonates directly. The honest read is contested and clock-dependent: security-denial at the frontier can succeed while the paradigm still 'fails' on diffusion, commercial value-capture, labor reinstatement (GV17), and the Global South standards contest simultaneously — no actor holds an uncontested path, and an open-weight capability floor plus the base rate of gradual adaptation both argue against any durable decisive advantage crystallizing at all.

## Binding constraint by actor
- **US:** Two distinct minimum staves on two clocks. PHYSICAL: GV6 (power & grid execution) — the buildout constraint with no export-control lever, taxed by China's GV8 materials/grid-equipment control. STRATEGIC/ECONOMIC: GV10 (open-weight + efficiency commoditization) erodes its frontier/market-protection position, and GV27 (China's second-pole credibility) which its whole chokepoint leverage depends on staying low. MONETARY: the GV37→GV38 pair — as open-capital-account issuer it cannot repress without losing the marginal UST bid, and SRF/SLR plumbing converts any supply overflow into forced monetization. The US controls chips (GV4) and holds them, but is the DEPENDENT party on minerals (GV8) and on electrons it cannot buy through.
- **China:** Minimum stave is GV1 (leading-edge logic fabrication) for FRONTIER TRAINING, gated behind GV2 (EUV/EDA tooling) and near-term GV3 (packaging/HBM) and GV5 (CUDA/interconnect scaling wall). This slow-clock (5-30yr) constraint is materially SOFTENED — not cleared — by three fast vectors it can supply itself: GV10 efficiency/open weights, GV4 circumvention, and GV12 talent return/extraction. Its monetary and energy endgames are NOT binding (closed capital account lets it repress rates; state-directed grid scales freely). This is also why its GV27 second-pole credibility is capped — the fab lock is the ceiling.
- **EU:** Minimum stave is GV12 (frontier talent) — NOT its own regulation (GV30), which it is actively hollowing. The equity-comp/tax wedge and researcher outflow gate capability more than rules do; energy cost (GV7) and grid (GV6) compound it, and it suffers strategic incoherence with no security anchor (GV24). Its ONE structural lever is a de-facto VETO in GV2 (ASML/Netherlands + Zeiss/Germany) — the tightest chokepoint node in the entire graph — plus regulatory rule-projection (Brussels Effect) it may not sustain.
- **Gulf:** Near-term minimum stave is the finance+security umbrella (folded in GV28) — dollar/SWIFT + CENTCOM basing is a harder coercion channel than compute (G42 divestiture precedent). Long-run binding ceiling is GV12 (talent/data flywheel) that capital cannot fully buy. Chip access (GV4) is permission-based and revocable. Its genuine assets — abundant unqueued power (a GV6 advantage) and sub-market capital (GV16) — make it a swing inference-supplier and neutral hub (GV28), but only while the GV27 auction stays two-sided and GV11 does not go winner-take-all; its risk is capturing hosting volume but not value (GV15).
