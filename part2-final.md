# Framework & Holistic Synthesis

My argument has three parts:
1. **Diffusion beats concentration:** frontier AI progress diffuses faster than it can concentrate — even in the case of a self-improving superintelligence.
2. **Value migrates from the model layer:** as the model layer commoditizes, value migrates in a barbell — downstream to the energy/hardware layer and upstream to distribution/services.
3. **China wins the battle of mercantilist constraints:** chips and EUV bind China today and matter most for *training* — but that lead diffuses. The binding constraint migrates to *inference and energy*, where the US is disadvantaged.

## Diffusion beats concentration
*[chart: capability vs diffusion]*

The key race is between AI capability improvement and the diffusion and commoditization of that intelligence. Capability is rising fast — METR's autonomous task-horizon at 50% reliability has gone from ~2 seconds to ~2.3 hours since 2019. But diffusion is faster: the Elo gap to the best open-weight model has collapsed from 250 in 2023 to 48 in 2026, and the lag from a closed state-of-the-art release to open-weight parity has fallen from ~24 months (GPT-3 in 2020 → OPT/BLOOM in 2022) to ~3 months by 2025 (Epoch AI). This is structural, not incidental, because (1) distillation is a one-way ratchet — followers copy an expensive teacher model at near-zero marginal cost — and (2) algorithmic-efficiency gains are non-excludable, leaking through publication, the internet, and a global labour market. The base rate for technological leads is that they are transient: the US held an atomic monopoly for just four years (1945–1949) despite total secrecy, an ocean of separation, and a physical-materials chokepoint tighter than anything in software — and the COCOM/Wassenaar regimes show even a *policy* chokepoint only delays, never denies. Even a self-improving Bostrom superintelligence would keep an unassailable lead only if (1) takeoff outpaced diffusion — hard when algorithms leak through the internet and labour mobility (the Klaus Fuchs channel) — and (2) the cognitive lead converted into a decisive resource/compute advantage, which is unlikely while the AI remains human-controlled: a US lab with a superintelligence is not going to bomb China's infrastructure or seize the world's compute for itself. **So we forecast the diffusion side wins — open weights stay within ~3 months / <50 Elo of the frontier through 2028 (F1, 80%); no system crosses the recursive-self-improvement threshold that would let a lead compound, i.e. human-expert parity on 32h+ ML-R&D tasks, by 2028 (F2, 72%); and open weights reach ≥40% of tokens by 2027 (F3, 65%).**

## Value migrates from the model layer
*[chart: the prize evaporates | open weights take over]*

Because diffusion outpaces capability, rent has drained from the model layer: the cost to reach a fixed (GPT-4-class) capability has fallen ~98% from 2020 to 2026, while open-weight models have taken a rising share of tokens — from 4% in 2023 to 33% in 2025 **(F4: blended GPT-4-class API price falls below $1/M tokens by end-2026, 70%)**. This open-base commoditization has precedent — MySQL/PostgreSQL erasing Oracle's database rent; RISC-V eroding ARM's licensing chokepoint. In each case the clever core commoditized and value moved off it in two directions. **Up-stack, into distribution and services:** Databricks built managed services on open-source Spark and thrived, while Cloudera — pure Hadoop distribution — failed; open-source Android protects Google's search, ads, and Play services ("commoditize your complement"). **Down-stack, into the physical layer:** TCP/IP commoditized and value moved into fibre and data centres; electrification's rent moved from the dynamo to the grid, utilities, and equipment makers (GE/Westinghouse); Standard Oil won by owning pipelines and rebates, not by refining better. We expect the same barbell here. Value accrues up-stack to the hyperscalers that own distribution and services (cloud plus consumer surfaces like Google Drive), and down-stack to whoever owns the proprietary physical standard — NVIDIA/CUDA, the Wintel of AI (the IBM-PC-clone pattern, in which the assembly commoditized and value concentrated in the proprietary standard), already at ~38× its 2019 data-centre revenue at ~73% gross margins; and TSMC, whose advanced-packaging capacity has climbed from 8k to 130k wafers/month between 2022 and 2026. **The corollary is a forecast that the model layer stops being a standalone business: a top-5 frontier lab ceases to operate as an independent model-API company — absorbed, acquired, or pivoted — by 2028 (F5, 55%).**

## China wins the battle of mercantilist constraints
*[chart: China's power ramp | US interconnection queue]*

As rent leaks from the model layer, the binding constraints become mercantilist. **China's** constraint is chips: NVIDIA's China revenue fell from 26% (FY2022) to 13% (FY2025); the BIS Entity List grew ~5.5× to ~1,300 entries between 2016 and 2025; and China cannot easily make its own leading edge — the Netherlands' ASML holds ~100% of sub-5nm EUV lithography, and SMIC's rapid indigenous DUV-only progress (28nm in 2015 to ~5nm-class in 2025) stalls at uneconomic ~30–40% yields, with no path further without EUV. But I forecast this gate does *not* bind China, for two reasons. First, the controls are porous — the COCOM/Wassenaar lesson is that export controls delay but do not deny, and an estimated 660k+ H100-equivalents reached China in 2025 anyway **(F9: US controls degrade to transactional — revenue-share or case-by-case licensing, or a net loosening — by 2028, 65%)**. Second, and more fundamentally, chips bind *training*, which sets the model frontier — and that frontier diffuses. Chips do not bind *inference*, where most of the economic value will accrue **(F6: China does not reach yield-viable EUV ≤5nm volume production by 2028, 80%)**.

**The US's** constraint is energy. The interconnection queue has grown ~5× since 2015 (to ~2,600 GW), and large-transformer / high-voltage lead times have widened to ~28–40 *months*, against data-centre demand rising from ~35 GW in 2025 to an estimated ~80 GW by 2030 — and a hardening political backlash: Gallup finds 71% of Americans oppose a data centre near them, understandable when PJM's independent market monitor attributed ~63% of the 2025/26 capacity-cost increase to data centres. China, by contrast, added ~540 GW of net capacity in 2025 versus the US's ~56 GW — and even that US figure was mostly solar, the wrong kind of power for 24/7 data-centre load **(F7: China's annual net power additions exceed 5× the US's in 2027, 85%; F8: ≥25% of announced US 2026–27 data-centre capacity is delayed, cancelled, or relocated by 2028, 70%)**. Energy has been decisive before — coal underwrote Britain's 18th–19th-century industrial revolution; oil abundance underwrote US hegemony in the 20th — but only when coupled with the absorptive capacity to convert it (the petrostate resource curse, and the energy-rich Soviet Union that still lost, are the counter-examples). China uniquely has both. (One long-shot wildcard on the US side: a SpaceX-style orbital data-centre build-out that bypasses the terrestrial grid entirely — but the physics of launch, cooling, and servicing make this a 2030s+ tail, not this-decade relief.)

**Liebig's law** holds that growth is set not by total resources but by the scarcest input. The core claim of this framework is that the binding minimum *migrates* — from China's EUV/chip constraint, which governs the fast-commoditizing training frontier, to the US's energy constraint, which governs the deployment phase where the durable value lives.

## Inversion
The page limit forces a focus on the base case (~55%), but four tail cases matter:
- **Perez-style demand/ROI bust (~20%):** as in the 2000–02 telecom/fibre crash, the crash is the turning point between the financial-capital-led installation frenzy and the deployment phase — a shakeout resolved by government industrial policy and re-regulation, plausibly against a late-stage Dalio debt cycle in which monetization and financial repression fund the backstop and accelerate dollar erosion **(F11: a major AI-buildout financial-stress event by 2027, 35%; F13: the dollar holds >50% of allocated reserves through 2028, 80%)**.
- **TSMC/Taiwan fracture (~13%):** a military action that throttles both US and Chinese progress at once by cutting leading-edge supply **(F10: no full kinetic invasion of Taiwan by 2028, 85%)**.
- **Superintelligence takeover (~12%):** a runaway AI, possibly outside human control — the one branch in which a lead *does* lock in (F2 is the gauge that would signal we are entering it).
- **Societal upheaval (cross-cutting):** AI-driven labour displacement on the scale of the industrial revolution's destruction of agricultural work, but faster — a risk that can accompany any branch **(F12: AI-attributable white-collar unemployment stays <2pp above trend through 2027, 65%)**.
