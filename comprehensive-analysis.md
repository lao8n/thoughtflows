## The overriding story

## The overriding story

This report makes one wager, and everything else hangs from it: **artificial intelligence is entering a contested plateau, not a takeoff — a regime in which raw capability commoditizes toward zero rent while the physical substrate that carries it hardens into the real moat, so that the actor who holds the intelligence frontier is, more often than not, *not* the actor who captures the value.** The two curves that define the age are already crossing on the same page. The price of a fixed unit of intelligence is in free-fall — GPT-4-launch-level capability cost \$30 per million input tokens in March 2023 and \$0.07 by Q4 2025, a collapse of roughly **40x per year**, with algorithmic efficiency halving about every eight months (the compute needed to hit a fixed capability fell from an index of 780 in 2018 to 0.15 in 2026). Against that, the lead-time to *move an electron or a transformer* is stretching: large power transformers went from a 12-month wait in 2018 to **40 months by 2026**, the US interconnection queue has ballooned past 2,600 GW, and median request-to-energization sits at 60 months. The prize evaporates; the moat widens. That single scissor is the thesis [chart: gt1_thesis.png].

The mechanism is a master-clock contest, and the clock has already been called. On one side sits the reinforcing recursive-self-improvement loop (R1) that the AGI-race worldview is built on — capability compounding into a self-closing, field-freezing lead. On the other sits the diffusion-and-commoditization flywheel (R3): every closed frontier advance leaking into open weights and cheaper inference until it erases the lead that produced it. **Diffusion is winning decisively, and it is not close.** Every RSI gauge reads sub-unity and stuck — METR's RE-bench shows AI agents at ~4x human experts on 2-hour ML-R&D tasks but collapsing to **0.5x at the 32-hour horizon**, the horizon that actually matters for automating research, and it has not moved. Meanwhile every diffusion gauge compounds: the open-to-closed capability gap has closed from 150 Elo (mid-2023) to ~45 (mid-2026), the diffusion lag has compressed from 26 months to **~2.5 months**, and open-weight models have gone from 4% of token usage in 2023 to **33% by late 2025**. This is the Linux/Android and MySQL-vs-Oracle pattern running at 10x speed: the base layer becomes a commodity and the proprietary rent retreats to the hosting, distribution, and infrastructure layers around it.

So the value follows the scarcity up-stack. As the model layer commoditizes — blended frontier API prices fell from \$60/M tokens (2020) to ~\$1.1 (2026), a ~95%+ collapse — durable rent migrates to what *can't* be forked overnight: leading-edge silicon, the power to run it, cloud aggregation, and the reorganized application layer that owns the customer and the workflow. This is the barbell [chart: gt4_value_capture.png]: combined hyperscaler capex is scaling from \$90B (2019) to a planned **\$700B (2026E)**, NVIDIA data-center revenue has run roughly 38x since FY2019 at 73% gross margins, and TSMC's CoWoS packaging capacity is up ~10x (8k→75k wafers/month, 2022–2025) — all while the intelligence those chips produce trends toward free. Standard Oil did not win by refining better; it won by owning the pipelines and rebates through which all oil had to flow. NVIDIA, the hyperscalers, and whoever ends up owning the grid interconnect are today's pipeline-and-rebate layer. Intelligence is becoming the kerosene — abundant, cheap, and someone else's margin.

The frontier holder is therefore frequently the value loser, and this is the report's most counter-intuitive, most load-bearing claim. Leading-edge chips bind **training but not inference** — the moat holds where models are *built* and leaks where they are *used* — and export controls tighten on hardware vectors (the BIS Entity List grew ~5.5x to ~1,300 entities; NVIDIA's China revenue share fell 26%→13%) while leaking on *outcomes* through open weights, cloud-API access, and 663k H100-equivalents' worth of diversion. A security-denial victory at the training frontier can thus coincide with total failure on diffusion, commercial value-capture, and labor reinstatement. The US can win the chip war and still watch the rent it was defending dissolve into open weights and Gulf-hosted inference. This is the COCOM/Wassenaar lesson — controls buy a 5–10 year *hardware* delay, not a durable capability monopoly — colliding with the VoIP-defeats-the-telecom-tariff lesson: a goods-based control regime is structurally porous to a services-based escape valve.

That is why the modal forecast is a **shape, not a race with one winner**: a messy, multipolar deployment under soft financial repression (base case, **P≈0.55**), in which power owners, compute owners, cloud aggregators, and the reorganized app layer split the rent and no single actor holds an uncontested path. It arrives via a turbulent Perez-style turning-point wobble — a partial shakeout, neither clean takeoff nor clean deployment — as a frenzy-phase capex mania (capex +63%/+73% YoY in 2024/2025) collides with two hard ceilings: the power/grid execution ceiling (B2) and the demand/ROI validation reckoning (B1, the revenue-per-token-vs-amortization gate). Around that base sit two discrete, un-averageable detonators and one bounded bust: a **Perez ROI bust** (P≈0.20) that flips the capex flywheel into an SPV-impairment cascade à la the 2000–02 telecom/fiber glut (bandwidth prices fell 99%+ post-crash); a **Taiwan/TSMC fracture** (P≈0.15) — the single discontinuity that zeroes both poles at once, cutting leading-edge supply *and* mineral/grid inputs together; and, held strictly apart and never blended into the average, the low-probability **RSI hard-takeoff** (P≈0.10) that would freeze the field into winner-take-all.

That last branch is where the AGI-race and export-control worldview lives — and we quarantine it deliberately. It is not wrong so much as *conditional on assumptions the 2026 evidence contradicts*: fast, compute-unconstrained, non-diffusing takeoff. Our takeoff analysis puts **P(unassailable | takeoff) at just 0.12**, because the doubling-count of a time-lead stays constant rather than growing against a ~3-month diffusion clock, because the compute/power substrate is shared and fab/grid-gated so a follower recurses on a copied frontier, and because weights, methods, and talent leak — the Klaus Fuchs channel — on the historical timescale. So we watch two gauges above all others: the RE-bench long-horizon crossing (32h+ sustained), whose flip alone switches on the quarantined winner-take-all world, and the revenue-per-token-vs-amortization gate, whose failure alone tips the base case into the bust. Until the first crosses unity, the diffusion clock runs the table — and the durable money is up-stack, in the electrons and the silicon, not in the intelligence itself.



## How to think about it: three layers, one coupled system

## How to think about it: three layers, one coupled system

The single most common analytical error in this field is to reference-class "AI" — to ask whether *the technology* is a bubble like fiber, a platform like Wintel, or a criticality event like 1945, and then argue about which analogy wins. That question has no answer because it is malformed. "AI" is not one process; it is a lattice of at least ten distinct feedback loops, each with its own physics, its own tempo, and its own correct reference class. The method here is to **reference-class each loop, not the technology** — and then to study how the loops are coupled.

So the compute-capex flywheel (R2) is reference-classed against the British Railway Mania of 1844–47 (≈7% of GDP/yr in authorized investment) and the 1996–2001 fiber glut, because those are the correct templates for a financial-capital surge building ahead of validated demand — and R2's gauges rhyme exactly: hyperscaler capex +63%/+73% YoY, NVIDIA data-center revenue up ~38x off its FY2021 base, CoWoS packaging ~10x since 2022. The diffusion flywheel (R3) is reference-classed against MySQL/PostgreSQL erasing Oracle's rent and RISC-V against ARM — the open-base commoditization pattern — and it too reads on-template: ~40x/yr price decline, algorithmic-efficiency halving every ~8 months, open-weight token share 4%→33% in three years, diffusion lag compressed from 26 months (2020) to ~2.5 (mid-2026). The RSI loop (R1) gets Bostrom's loop-gain theory and nuclear criticality (1945) as its *only* honest analogy — a genuine physical threshold, not a trend. The chip moat (R4) gets COCOM→Wassenaar→BIS (which historically buys ~5–10 years of delay, no more). Each loop, its own history.

### Three registers per loop

Every loop is then read in three registers, and a claim only counts when the registers agree:

1. **Theory template** — the mechanism that says *why* the loop should behave as it does (Perez's frenzy→turning-point, Bostrom's loop-gain, Acemoglu–Restrepo displacement-vs-reinstatement, Dalio's big-cycle).
2. **Historical instance** — a dated, resolved case that already ran the mechanism to completion (railway mania and its 1847 crash; the LISP-machine/expert-systems AI winter of 1984–93; the 2010 rare-earth embargo that proved the mineral weapon is short and reversible).
3. **Live gauge** — the numeric series we can watch *now* to see whether this instance is tracking that template ([chart: gt3_master_derivative.png]).

Theory without a live gauge is astrology; a gauge without a template is noise. Requiring all three is what disciplines the forecast.

### Prediction lives in thresholds, not derivatives

The derivatives tell you which loop is *winning right now* — and today they are unambiguous. Every RSI gauge is sub-unity and stuck (METR RE-bench: AI ~4x human experts at 2-hour horizons but ~0.5x at 32 hours; SWE-bench Verified stalling ~77–81%), while every diffusion gauge compounds. So diffusion (R3) is beating recursion (R1) decisively as of mid-2026. But the *forecast* does not live in the slope; it lives at the **named flip-thresholds** where a loop's gain crosses a line and the regime itself changes — RE-bench crossing unity on sustained 32-hour ML-R&D horizons (ignites Branch B); revenue-per-token falling below infra amortization at scale for 2–3 sustained quarters (flips R2 into B1, Branch C); China indigenizing EUV at volume in the 2026–28 window (voids R4). Derivatives earn conviction about the present; thresholds define the future. We track the first to place bets on the second.

### Substrate plus discrete branches — nested, never averaged

The loops sit inside three nested clocks that must not be blended into one number. The **slow** Dalio big-cycle (late-top reserve-currency phase, fiscal-dominance drift, r* upward-sticky, dollar ~58% of reserves) is the substrate — it sets the cost of capital and the geopolitical stakes. The **medium** Perez surge (R2 dominant, B1 latent) sets the tempo and the coming turning-point test. The **fast** Bostrom loop is quarantined and open. The outer clock bounds the inner; averaging them would smear a $700B-capex frenzy, a bond-market regime, and a criticality tail into a meaningless mean.

The output is therefore **a shape plus quarantined tails, not a point estimate**: Branch A, messy multipolar up-stack deployment under soft repression (0.55); Branch C, a Perez ROI bust (0.20); Branch D, a Taiwan fracture that zeroes both poles at once (0.15); and — held strictly apart, never averaged in because its payoff is unbounded — Branch B, RSI takeoff (0.10). The rest of this report walks the lattice loop by loop, and the two gauges to watch above all others are the RE-bench long-horizon crossing and the revenue-per-token/amortization gate: the first switches on the quarantined world, the second tips the base case into the bust.



## The engine: the feedback loops

## The engine: the feedback loops

Do not read the systems-map as a menu of risks. Read it as one machine with six moving linkages, all bolted to a single crankshaft — the frontier-capability variable at **GV11** — and turning against two clocks (the diffusion clock and the takeoff clock) inside a late-cycle monetary casing. The loops are not independent; they share nodes, share sign-flips, and hand torque to each other. What follows walks each linkage — mechanism, the live gauge that reads it *with its number*, and the threshold at which it flips sign — and then shows the coupling that makes them one system.

### R2 — the compute–capex flywheel (the engine that is actually turning)

**Mechanism.** Expected ROI on frontier AI → hyperscaler capex → orders for accelerators and advanced packaging → NVIDIA/TSMC revenue → reinvestment and capacity → more capability → higher expected ROI. This is the loop literally moving trillions right now, and every other loop in the system is currently *reacting* to the buildout it drives.

**Live gauge.** Combined hyperscaler capex (MSFT+GOOG+AMZN+META) ran **$256B (2024) → $443B (2025) → ~$700B (2026E)**, i.e. **+63% YoY in 2024, +73% in 2025**; NVIDIA data-center revenue went **$3B (FY2019) → $47.5B (FY2024)**, roughly **38×**; TSMC CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity climbed **8k → 130k wafers/month (2022→2026)**, ~**16×**. The flywheel is not decelerating — it is accelerating hard [chart: gt4_value_capture.png].

**Flip-threshold.** R2 does not slow of its own accord; it is flipped *from outside* at the demand-validation gate **GV14** — when **revenue-per-token stays below infrastructure amortization at scale for ~2–3 sustained quarters**. At that point R2 inverts into B1 (below). The historical template for that inversion is the 1996–2001 fiber buildout, where ~$1T of capex met a demand curve that arrived late and prices collapsed 99%+.

This is the **system-dominant loop**: it sets the tempo, the stakes, and the capital-at-risk. Everything else is either the surface it grinds against or the counter-force eroding what it produces.

### R3 — the diffusion / commoditization flywheel (the brake that is winning)

**Mechanism.** Frontier capability, once demonstrated, leaks — via open weights, algorithmic-efficiency gains, and price competition — until the model layer is a commodity and the lead evaporates. This is R2's pervasive erosive counter-force: R2 builds the frontier, R3 gives it away.

**Live gauge (GV10).** Every diffusion gauge compounds: **$/capability falling ~40×/yr** (GPT-4-launch capability **$30/M input tokens (Mar-2023) → $0.07 (Q4-2025)**); algorithmic efficiency halving **~every 8 months** (relative compute to a fixed capability **100 in 2020 → 0.15 by 2026**); the open-vs-closed Elo gap **closing 150 → ~45–55 points (2023→2026)**; diffusion lag compressing **26 months (2020) → ~2.5–3 months (2026)**; open weights now **~33% of token usage (late-2025)**, up from 4% in 2023 [chart: gt3_master_derivative.png]. The analogy is exact: **MySQL/PostgreSQL + the LAMP stack vs Oracle/DB2** — the commodity base wins the cost-sensitive majority of the market and rent migrates to hosting/distribution.

**Flip-threshold.** R3 is only defeated if a **decisive-capability threshold at GV11 crosses *before* commoditization completes (pre-2032)** — i.e. if R1 (below) beats the clock. Absent that, diffusion runs the table.

### B2 — the power/grid execution ceiling (the physical brake)

**Mechanism.** R2's capex can only cash out as deployed compute if electrons, transformers, and interconnection show up. They aren't. This is the true **US binding stave**: a queue-and-transformer ceiling that no amount of frenzy capital can buy through on frenzy timescales.

**Live gauge (GV6).** US interconnection queue **~2,600 GW (2023)** of capacity waiting; request-to-COD time **stuck at ~55–60 months**; HV transformer lead time **28 → 33 → 40 weeks (2024→2026)**, i.e. **+10–15 wk/yr and widening**; US HV transmission additions **stuck ~450–888 mi/yr** against data-center demand accelerating **35 GW (2025) → 80 GW (2030)**. The asymmetry is the story: China added **540 GW of net capacity in 2025** to America's tens [chart: gt2_us_power_bind.png]. The structural analogy is **Alcoa's aluminum energy-geography lock-in (1886–1945)** — compute migrates to where cheap firm power already is.

**Flip-threshold.** Relief requires **interconnection reform + behind-the-meter gas/nuclear + SMRs clearing the queue faster than load grows** — partial by ~2028, not before. Until then B2 caps how fast R2 converts dollars into deployed FLOPs, and it is *binding and tightening* today.

### R4 — the chip-chokepoint / mercantilist ratchet (with B3 as the counter-lever)

**Mechanism.** Export controls on leading-edge tooling and accelerators deny the challenger the substrate for frontier *training*, buying time; China answers with a **minerals counter-lever (B3)** — the ability to tax the buildout through rare-earth/gallium/magnet chokepoints. Two ratchets pulling opposite directions on the same buildout.

**Live gauge.** **GV4:** cumulative Chinese entities on the BIS Entity List **236 (2016) → ~1,300 (2025)**, ~5.5×; NVIDIA China revenue share **26% → 13% (FY2022→FY2025)**; but leakage compounds — Singapore-billed revenue **7% → 22%**, smuggled H100-equivalents **~5k (2022) → ~663k (2025, central estimate incl. undetected)**, Huawei Ascend shipments **30k → 400k (2022→2025)**. **B3 (GV8):** China rare-earth processing **stuck ~87–91%**, gallium **$298/kg (2020) → $2,269/kg (2025)**, ~7–9×; NdFeB magnet share **94% (2024)**; magnet exports whipsawing **4,846 → 1,238 → 3,188 t/month** through 2025 as the switch is flicked. The template is the **2010 rare-earth embargo on Japan**: the weapon is short and reversible in months but durable as a deterrent.

**Flip-thresholds.** R4 voids if **China indigenizes EUV at volume (GV2 prototype→production, ~2026–28 window)** — note the moat binds *training, not inference* (couples at GV1). B3 is removed only when **ex-China processing reaches ~30–40% (post-2028)**; MP Materials at **2,599 t NdPr (2025) → 10,000 t target (2028)** is the clock. The deeper lesson from **VoIP defeating PTT hardware regulation** and **RISC-V vs ARM**: a goods-based control regime is structurally defeated by the cloud-API/inference leg and by adversarial open-sourcing.

### B1 + B5 — the demand/ROI and labor-reinstatement brakes (the two ways R2 starves)

**Mechanism.** R2 needs its output *bought*. Two demand-side brakes can starve it: **B1**, capital fragility — revenue-per-token failing to clear infra amortization, cascading through neocloud/SPV vendor-financing structures; and **B5**, the underconsumption brake — if AI displaces labor faster than it reinstates it, the wage-share erodes, MPC-differential bites, and the demand that was supposed to validate the capex never materializes (Acemoglu–Restrepo task-displacement running ahead of reinstatement).

**Live gauge.** **B1 is latent, not yet triggered:** hyperscalers are insulated by **$50B+/yr free cash flow** and self-fund; the fragility sits one tier down in neocloud/SPV structures (GV16). **B5 is latent and indeterminate:** entry-level knowledge-work contraction and apprenticeship-shortening are visible; the reinstatement ratio (GV17) is not yet cleanly sub-unity. Both are *coiled*, not sprung.

**Flip-thresholds.** B1 flips when **revenue-per-token < infra amortization, sustained ~2–3 quarters at scale** → SPV impairment cascade (this *is* the branch-C bust, P=0.20; the telecom/fiber 2000–02 template). B5 flips when the **reinstatement ratio holds <1 + the consumer-credit buffer exhausts + the MPC differential bites**. Either brake, engaged, transmits straight back into R2 as collapsing expected ROI.

### B4 — the fiscal-dominance / bond-market doom-loop (the monetary casing)

**Mechanism.** The whole machine runs inside a late-Dalio reserve-currency envelope. Deficits and debt-service drift force the monetary regime toward soft repression, which sets the cost of capital that R2's ROI math is discounted at. If AI productivity arrives *contemporaneously*, deficits self-liquidate and the loop is benign; if it lags, the bond market does the tightening and starves the flywheel of cheap capital.

**Live gauge.** **r\* upward-sticky**; dollar share of reserves **~58% and slowly eroding**; Treasury-plumbing stress (GV38, basis-trade/dealer-balance-sheet) episodic; the productivity offset (GV40) **not yet visible** — capex is contemporaneous, TFP is not. Regime is *drifting toward soft repression*, not yet at realized repression.

**Flip-threshold.** Benign flip: **AI productivity offset (GV40) arrives contemporaneously → deficits self-liquidate.** Malign ratchet: **GV38 non-linear plumbing stress forces realized financial repression** (tail P=0.25). This is the **1844–47 Railway Mania → Bank Charter Act suspension → Baring** template — a capital-export mania meeting a monetary boundary.

## The master switch: RSI vs diffusion, and why the lead probably doesn't lock in

## The master switch: RSI vs diffusion, and why the lead probably doesn't lock in

Everything else in this report — the power bind, the ROI reckoning, the Taiwan tail, the up-stack migration of rent — is a story about *degree* and *distribution*. This section is the one about *kind*. At gate GV11 sits the single coupling that decides whether the AI transition is a diffusion event that many actors survive or a discontinuity that one actor wins outright. Two loops pull the same variable — frontier capability — in opposite directions, and their contest is the master switch of the entire system.

### Two loops, one variable

**R1, the RSI reinforcing loop**, is Bostrom's recursive self-improvement engine: AI that is good enough at ML research to accelerate the building of the next AI, whose loop gain, if it crosses unity, closes on itself and compounds endogenously. Above the crossover, a time-lead becomes a capability chasm, the field freezes, and the holder of the lead *through* the threshold wins a decisive strategic advantage. This is branch B — quarantined, never averaged into the base case, because its payoff structure is categorically different from everything else.

**R3, the diffusion/commoditization flywheel**, is the balancing counter-force: every capability the frontier discovers leaks — into open weights, into cheaper inference, into re-derived methods — faster than any single lab can fence it off. Where R1 wants to concentrate, R3 wants to disperse.

The coupling at GV11 carries a probability weight of **P=0.72 that diffusion dominates now**, and the gauges are not close. [chart: gt3_master_derivative.png] shows the tension in one frame: the METR 50%-task-completion horizon is genuinely accelerating (0.03 min in 2019 → 5 min in early 2023 → 39 min Dec 2024 → 137 min by Aug 2025), while the open-weight diffusion lag has collapsed from 26 months (2020) to 13 (2023) to 3 (2025) and is now flooring out around 2.5 months — a floor, not a cliff. Capability climbs; the leak-time barely compresses further. That is a diffusion regime, and it is compounding:

| Diffusion gauge (R3) | Trajectory | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| $/capability (GPT-4-level input) | ~40×/yr decline | $30/M (Mar-23) → $0.07/M (late-25) |
| Algorithmic efficiency (compute to fixed capability) | halving ~every 8 mo | 780 (2018) → 100 (2020) → 1.2 (2024) → 0.15 (2026), index 2020=100 |
| Open-weight token-usage share | accelerating | 4% (2023) → 22% (mid-25) → 33% (late-25) |

Against this, **every RSI gauge reads sub-unity and stuck.** The decisive one is METR RE-bench: AI-agent vs human-expert on frontier ML R&D. At short horizons AI is superhuman (~4× at 30 min, ~4× at 2 h), but the ratio *decays with task length* — 1.2× at 8 h, **0.5× at 32 h** — and those are the November 2024 readings that have not budged. The loop that matters for RSI is exactly the long-horizon one, because self-improvement is a 32-hour-plus research task, not a 2-hour benchmark. SWE-bench Verified tells the same story of a top that is flattening as it approaches saturation: 1.7% (Oct-23) → 49% → 74.9% → 77.2% → 80.8% (Apr-26). Capability is high and rising; the specific capability that would close R1 is not yet in evidence. Diffusion is winning, decisively, today.

So the whole question reduces to **timing**: does a decisive-capability threshold cross *before* commoditization completes (our marker: pre-2032), or does the diffusion clock run the table? The base case (branch A, P=0.55) is that no durable threshold crosses by 2028 and probably not by 2030 — messy, multipolar deployment in which rent migrates up-stack and the frontier holder is often not the value winner. Branch B — the takeoff — sits at **P=0.10**, quarantined. The rest of this section is the anatomy of that 0.10, and why even conditional on a takeoff the lead probably still doesn't lock in.

### The takeoff deep-dive: by what mechanism could a lead become unassailable?

Bostrom's argument is internally valid but load-bearing on three assumptions, each of which the 2026 evidence contradicts: that takeoff is **fast**, that it is **compute-unconstrained**, and that it is **non-diffusing**. An unassailable lead requires all three at once. We assess **P(unassailable | takeoff) = 0.12** — meaning that even if R1 crosses unity, the follower still catches up seven times out of eight. Decompose the lock-in channels:

| Lock-in channel | P | The bounding history |
|---|---|---|
| Fast-takeoff crossover (time-lead → capability chasm) | 0.25 | **Nuclear criticality (1945)** — the closest real "crossover," a genuine physical threshold; yet the monopoly still eroded in ~4 years |
| Optimization-power / recalcitrance (leader recurses *faster*, gap widens) | 0.20 | **Moore's-law learning curves** — the process leader (Intel) compounded but never ran away; returns were constant-to-diminishing, not explosive |
| Cognitive lead → resource/compute/control lead (non-kinetic) | 0.30 | **Standard Oil** — cornered a physical resource, took *decades*, and was broken by antitrust |
| Wealth → capability compounding (revenue funds compute funds capability) | 0.20 | **Google Search / Windows** — real, durable *market* leadership; never *world domination* |
| Singleton formation (one global decision agency) | 0.10 | **No singleton has ever formed** — Rome, Pax Britannica, the Cold War US all fell short |

The pattern is diagnostic. The channels that are *most real* (cognitive→resource, wealth→capability) are precisely the ones that are *most bounded*: they reliably produce coercive economic-market leadership, and just as reliably fail to produce winner-take-all planetary control. The channels that would deliver true unassailability (recalcitrance-driven gap-widening, singleton) require increasing returns and a kinetic or governance monopoly that the historical record has never once produced under human control.

### The head-start math

Work the lead in doublings. A one-year lead is worth `(12 / doubling-time-in-months)` doublings of capability. The decisive question is not the *size* of that number but whether it **grows or stays constant** relative to the diffusion lag.

- If returns to software R&D are **r ≤ 1** — constant or diminishing — the doubling time is constant or lengthening, and a one-year lead is a *fixed multiplicative* gap. It is persistent but bounded, and diffusion competes it down. The observed dynamics (METR long-horizon ratio flat at 0.5×, SWE-bench flattening near saturation) are squarely in this regime.
- Only if returns are **r > 1** — hyperbolic, with doubling time *falling below the diffusion lag and continuing to accelerate* — does the doubling-count grow faster than the frontier leaks, and the gap explode into a chasm. That is the unassailable case, and it needs software-only recursion with low recalcitrance *and* exclusive compute.

This is why the physical substrate is the executioner of the fast case. RSI does not run in a vacuum; it runs on fabs and electrons. As long as leading-edge training compute is shared and fab/grid-gated — TSMC at ~90% of sub-5nm capacity, CoWoS packaging the binding constraint, and the US power ceiling (B2) throttling every actor's marginal compute (interconnection queues at ~2,300 GW, transformer lead times pushing 40 months) — the follower recurses on a *copied frontier without an exclusive-compute disadvantage*. The leader cannot starve the follower's parallel recursion when they draw from the same silicon and the same grid. **Any one** of three conditions collapses the lead to bounded: r ≤ 1, shared compute substrate, or weights/methods/talent diffusing on the historical 3–6-month clock. All three currently hold. Unassailability needs all three to fail simultaneously — hence 0.12.

### Theory meets the base rate

Bostrom's clean crossover has exactly one strong historical analogue, and it cuts *against* him. Nuclear weapons in 1945 were the genuine article: a physical criticality threshold, a decisive military capability, a monopoly holder. And the monopoly **eroded in roughly four years** — the USSR tested in 1949 — because knowledge diffused through human vectors faster than the physical artifact could be fenced. The Klaus Fuchs / Theodore Hall espionage network compressed the Soviet timeline directly; tacit and explicit method leaked despite the most extreme security regime a state has ever mounted. If the closest crossover in history — a literal physical threshold guarded by wartime secrecy — leaked in four years, an AI lead defended by open-weight release cycles, published methods, and a talent pool that changes employers for eight-figure packages will leak faster. The 2.5-month diffusion lag is the modern Fuchs.

The softer analogues confirm the bounded outcome. **Standard Oil** shows that cornering even a purely physical resource takes decades and ends in antitrust, not permanence. **Google and Windows** show that data-and-revenue flywheels *do* compound and persist — into durable *market* dominance, never into a singleton. The nearest approaches to global political monopoly — Rome, Pax Britannica, the postwar US nuclear monopoly itself — all fell short and then reversed. History has produced persistent leaders many times and an unassailable one never.

### Calibrated probability and what to watch

We hold two numbers strictly apart and never average them:

- **Branch B (RSI takeoff), P = 0.10** — the probability that R1 crosses unity and the takeoff *happens*. This is the quarantined, unbounded-payoff tail.
- **P(unassailable | takeoff) = 0.12** — the probability that, conditional on a takeoff, the lead actually locks into a winner-take-all field freeze rather than being competed back down.

Their product, ~**0.012**, is the probability of the genuine Bostrom endgame — a single actor holding a durable decisive strategic advantage. It is small not because takeoff is impossible but because the two brakes — the **diffusion clock** (R3, compounding) and the **physical/compute substrate** (shared fabs, the B2 power ceiling) — are individually sufficient to break the lock-in, and both are currently binding.

This is the one gauge to watch above all others, because it is the switch that would rewrite the base case rather than perturb it. The trigger is unambiguous and quarantined: **METR RE-bench / SWE-bench AI-vs-human-expert crossing unity on sustained 32-hour-plus frontier ML-R&D horizons.** Until that long-horizon ratio moves off 0.5×, R1 stays open and sub-unity, diffusion keeps winning, and the modal world remains messy multipolar deployment. Watch the derivative in [chart: gt3_master_derivative.png]: the day the diffusion-lag floor starts *rising* while the task-horizon curve steepens is the day the master switch begins to flip — and not one quarter before.

### The frontier moat: GV1, GV2, GV4, GV21 — holds at the training edge, leaks everywhere else

Four gates decide whether the leading-edge hardware advantage is a durable wall or a temporary speed bump. Verdict of the cluster: the wall holds for *training* the frontier, but it is being routed around on *outcomes*.

**GV2 — EUV / EDA tooling chokepoint (the gate behind the fab gate)** *(hard_physical)* — [chart: gate_GV2.png]

The hardest gate on the board. ASML holds **~100%** of sub-5nm-capable EUV lithography tooling (94% in a single 2024 datapoint, otherwise 100% across 2016–2025) and shipped 4 systems/yr (2016) → 26 (2019) → **44 (2024)**. **GV1 — Leading-edge logic fabrication** *(soft_slow)* — [chart: gate_GV1.png]

The near-term binding constraint is not wafers but *packaging*: TSMC CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity went 8k wafers/mo (2022) → 38k (2024) → 75k (2025) → a planned **130k (2026)** — a ~10x ramp — and China WFE spend hit **~$49B/yr** (2024–25). US diversification is real but late: TSMC Arizona sub-5nm went 0 (2022) → 10k wafers/mo (2024) → **95k (2025)** → 150k (2028E). **Mechanism:** you can force nodes without EUV, but yield economics punish you — the DUV multi-patterning workaround produces chips at uneconomic cost. **Past→future:** by 2028 SMIC produces 5nm-class in *low volume at ~30–40% yield*, maybe demos 3nm on DUV, but not at US-competitive economics. **Analogy:** *Nikon/Canon vs ASML* (partial mid-range catch-up, no frontier close) and the *GE/P&W/Rolls-Royce single-crystal turbine-blade oligopoly* (1960s–present), which calibrates the catch-up clock for tacit-knowledge manufacturing at decades, not years. **Verdict: soft_slow** — leading-edge chips bind *training* but not *inference*, so the moat is real at the frontier and porous everywhere downstream.

## Does the gate bind what matters? Second-order chains

## Does the gate bind what matters? Second-order chains

The chip gate is the most-watched control valve in the system — and it is largely aimed at the wrong outcome. The first-order story is clean: BIS controls hold, the Entity List runs ~5.5x from 236 (2016) to ~1,300 (2025), NVIDIA's China revenue share collapses 26%→13%, and ASML remains ~100% of sub-5nm EUV tooling with China's indigenous light source at 125W against a frontier requirement it cannot yet meet [chart: gate_GV4.png]. On the hardware vector, the coalition is winning. But a gate is only as valuable as the thing it binds, and four second-order chains — substitution, scope-of-constraint, value-locus-shift, and asymmetric adoption — each show a hardening gate failing to bind the value-capturing outcome. This is the analytic core of the model's synthesis: **security-denial at the frontier can succeed while the paradigm still "fails" on diffusion, commercial value-capture, and labor reinstatement simultaneously.** The winner of the physical-frontier contest is frequently not the winner of the value contest.

### 1. Scope-of-constraint: the gate binds TRAINING, not INFERENCE

This is the load-bearing distinction, and it is explicit in the coupling map. **@GV1 (scope_of_constraint, P=0.85): leading-edge chips bind TRAINING, not INFERENCE.** The R2 compute–capex flywheel and the R4 chip moat both operate in the *training* regime — you need the newest sub-5nm silicon, the CoWoS packaging (ramping ~10x, 8k→75k wafers/month 2022→2025, heading to 130k in 2026), and the exclusive-compute substrate to *create* frontier capability. The moat is real there: TSMC holds ~90% of ≤5nm capacity and the frontier marches 5nm(2020)→3nm(2022)→2nm(2025) [chart: gate_GV1.png].

But almost none of the *economic value* of AI is realized in training. It is realized in inference — in *running* the model billions of times against real workloads. And inference does not require the leading edge. It runs on N-1 and N-2 silicon, on Huawei Ascend parts (30k→400k units 2022→2025 target), on rented capacity, on last generation's depreciated fleet. The historical template is named directly in GV4: **VoIP/Skype circumventing PTT hardware-based telecom regulation (1995–2015) — the cloud-API/inference-as-a-service leg structurally defeats a goods-based control.** You can embargo the fab; you cannot embargo the *output* of the model, which arrives as tokens over an API. So a control regime that clamps down harder and harder on the training frontier is squeezing the part of the value chain where rent is *least* durable, and leaving untouched the part — inference, deployment, application — where the economy-wide value actually accrues.

### 2. Substitution: distillation and efficiency route around the gate

Even within capability-creation, the gate leaks on *outcomes* while it tightens on *hardware*. **@GV4 (substitution, P=0.65): R4 tightens the export-control bite while R3 says open weights + algorithmic efficiency route around it.** The diffusion clock (GV10) is the substitution engine:

| Gauge (GV10/GV11) | 2023 | 2025–26 | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| $/GPT-4-class capability (input tokens) | $30/M | $0.07/M | ~40x/yr decline |
| Algorithmic efficiency (compute to fixed capability, 2020=100) | 11 (2022) | 0.15 (2026) | halving ~every 8mo |
| Open-weight vs closed gap (LMArena Elo) | 150 | 45 (mid-2026) | closing |
| Diffusion lag (SOTA→open-weight) | 26mo (2020) | 2.5–3mo | floored ~3mo |
| Open-weight token share | 4% | 33% (late 2025) | compounding |

Distillation is the substitution mechanism made concrete: a follower with restricted compute does not need to re-run the frontier training campaign; it distills a smaller model off a leaked or API-accessible frontier model, and algorithmic efficiency (halving every ~8 months) does the rest. A chip gate that buys the historically-calibrated **~5–10 years of frontier-training delay (COCOM→Wassenaar→BIS analogy)** buys nothing against a capability that re-derives itself in 2.5 months of diffusion lag. The relevant historical warning is **Klaus Fuchs / Manhattan Project espionage (1942–1949): knowledge transfer through human and informational vectors compresses the follower's timeline regardless of the physical-materials monopoly.** The gate holds the atoms; the bits leak. And the smuggling channel confirms even the atoms leak at the margin — documented H100-equivalent diversion runs 5k(2022)→~300k(2025 documented), with a central estimate including undetected of ~663k [chart: gate_GV4.png].

### 3. Value-locus-shift: value in economy-wide use ≠ value in intelligence-creation

The deepest reason the gate does not bind what matters is that it targets *intelligence-creation* while durable value migrates to *economy-wide use* and to the physical layers underneath. **@GV15 (value_locus_shift, P=0.7): rent migrates UP-STACK, not away — as the model layer commoditizes (API price −95%), rent accrues to hardware, hyperscaler cloud, and the reorganized application layer.** [chart: gt4_value_capture.png]

The barbell is stark. Hyperscaler capex runs $90B(2019)→$443B(2025)→~$700B(2026E) and NVIDIA DC revenue compounds ~$2.9B(FY19)→$47.5B(FY24) at ~73% gross margin — while the blended frontier API price collapses $60(2020)→~$1.1(2026) per 1M tokens [chart: gate_GV15.png]. The model layer — precisely the thing the chip gate is designed to protect a lead in — is the layer being *commoditized to zero rent.* The controlling analogy is named in GV15: **Linux/Android/cloud open-base pattern — commoditized software, proprietary rent captured at the hosting/distribution layer**, reinforced by **MySQL/PostgreSQL vs Oracle (1995–2018)** and the **Wintel** reorganization. The rent does not disappear; it moves to where the chokepoints are physical and non-diffusing: power, compute, cloud aggregation, and the application layer that owns the customer.

This is why the model's base case (branch A, P=0.55) says wins are "distributed and contested: owners of power, compute, cloud aggregation." An actor can *win the chip war and lose the value war* — hold the training frontier through export controls while the durable rent pools in electrons and cloud gravity it does not necessarily control. The true US binding stave is not chips but power (B2): interconnection queue ~2.6TW, request-to-COD ~60 months, LPT lead time +10–15wk/yr to ~40 months by 2026, against China adding 540GW/yr [chart: gt2_us_power_bind.png]. The **electrolytic-aluminum / Alcoa energy-geography lock-in** analogy predicts value migrates to where power is cheap and buildable — a gate the chip regime does not touch at all.

### 4. Asymmetric adoption: the gate's value depends on the follower staying non-credible

**@GV27 (asymmetric_adoption, P=0.55): genuinely contested — US access-leverage holds only while China stays a non-credible outside option.** A chokepoint is leverage only if the target has no substitute. The moment Huawei Ascend + SMIC 5nm-class + a domestic open-weight stack become a *good-enough* alternative for inference and mid-tier training, the gate stops coercing and starts *accelerating* indigenization — the **RISC-V vs ARM** dynamic named in GV10, where adversarial open-sourcing becomes national-security strategy. SMIC reaching 5nm-class (2025-12), China domestic WFE at 35% and targeting 80% by 2030, EDA self-sufficiency climbing 5%→13% — each notch erodes the gate's coercive value even before it "fails" on the frontier. The gate can hold physically (GV2: ASML ~100% of EUV through 2028, >90% probability) and still lose its *leverage* because the follower's adoption path routes around the constrained node.

### Where the hardening gate does NOT bind

Synthesizing the four chains against the master-clock contest at GV11 — R1 (RSI, self-closing takeoff) vs R3 (diffusion, lead-erasing) — the verdict is unambiguous in the present regime. Every RSI gauge reads sub-unity and stuck: METR RE-bench shows AI ~4x human experts at 2h but ~0.5x at 32h horizons, and that 32h figure is *not moving* [chart: gt3_master_derivative.png]. Every diffusion gauge compounds. **Diffusion is winning now**, which means the chip gate is defending a lead that the diffusion clock erases faster than the gate can widen it.

The gate binds only in the quarantined tail. In branch B (P=0.10, held strictly apart), if the RE-bench 32h horizon crosses unity *before* commoditization completes (pre-2032), R1 self-closes, diffusion freezes, and exclusive compute on a shared fab/grid substrate becomes decisive — the follower's parallel recursion starves. *There*, and essentially only there, does the training-frontier gate bind the value-capturing outcome; and even then P(unassailable | takeoff) is only ~0.12, because the lead must compound endogenously faster than the historical ~3–6 month diffusion clock leaks it. Nuclear criticality (1945) is the sole clean "crossover" analogy, and it is load-bearing on an unproven discontinuity.

In the modal world, the chain of failure is complete: **the gate binds training (not inference), can be substituted-around by distillation and efficiency, targets a commoditizing layer while rent migrates up-stack to power/compute/cloud/apps, and holds leverage only while the follower stays non-credible.** A perfectly enforced chip embargo is therefore consistent with a paradigm that still delivers no killer app, no ROI validation, no labor reinstatement, and no durable model-layer rent. Security-denial and value-capture are decoupled. Watch the RE-bench 32h crossing — the *only* switch that re-couples them — and the revenue-per-token/amortization gate, which decides whether the base case tips into the Perez bust regardless of how tightly the chips are held.

## The reference classes: this has happened before

## The reference classes: this has happened before

No part of the current moment is unprecedented in kind — only in scale and in the simultaneity of the clocks. The value of the historical corpus is not decoration; it is calibration. Each named episode below is a *measured* prior on one of the loops in the systems map, and read together they tell us which mechanisms lock a lead in, which merely rent one, and which reliably blow up. The three timescales — Bostrom-fast, Perez-medium, Dalio-slow — nest, and the outer bounds the inner. We take them in that order.

### The medium clock: Perez, and why we are in *late frenzy*, not deployment

Carlota Perez's model of techno-economic surges runs installation → frenzy → turning-point (crash) → deployment (golden age). The diagnostic markers of *frenzy* are financial capital decoupling from production, asset valuations running on expected rather than realized returns, and a physical buildout racing ahead of validated demand. Every one of those markers is lit. Combined hyperscaler capex (Microsoft + Google + Amazon + Meta) ran **$256B in 2024 → $443B in 2025 → ~$700B guided for 2026**, growth of **+63% then +73% YoY** [chart: gt4_value_capture.png]. NVIDIA data-center revenue went from **$3.0B (FY2020) to $47.5B (FY2024)** — the ~38x engine of loop R2, the compute-capex flywheel that is the *system-dominant* loop right now. TSMC CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity, the true near-term bottleneck, expanded **8kwpm (2022) → 75kwpm (2025) → 130kwpm (2026E)**, ~10x in four years [chart: gate_GV1.png]. This is a canonical financial-capital mania on *expected* ROI.

The reference class for how such a buildout resolves is unambiguous and it is a **buildout-bust**, not a smooth golden age:

| Episode | Capital deployed | What overshot | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **British Railway Mania, 1844–47** | >£200M authorized, ~7% of GDP/yr at peak | Route miles on speculative traffic forecasts | Overend Gurney (1866), Baring (1890); rails survived, equity holders did not |
| **Telecom/fiber glut, 1996–2003** | ~$1T global fiber | Bandwidth on internet-traffic hockey-sticks | Prices −99%+ post-2001; capacity real, financiers wiped |
| **Merchant-power / Enron, 1996–2002** | Deregulated gas-turbine overbuild | Generation on merchant-price forecasts | Impairment cascade, Enron collapse |

The transferable lesson is precise: **the physical asset survives the financing that built it, and the frontier-builder is frequently not the value-capturer.** Fiber laid in 1999 carried the 2010s cloud boom — but at prices that bankrupted the layers. This is exactly the systems map's base case (Branch A, ~0.55): rent migrates *up-stack* to power, compute, cloud aggregation, and the reorganized application layer, while the model layer commoditizes underneath. The Linux/Android and Wintel precedents sharpen it — an open, commoditizing base layer with proprietary rent captured one level up at hosting/distribution. Blended frontier API price has already fallen **$60/1M tokens (2020) → $1.1 (2026)** even as hyperscaler capex quintupled: the barbell of the whole thesis [chart: gt1_thesis.png].

The bust scenario (Branch C, ~0.20) is the telecom 2000–02 template applied to the neocloud/SPV financing tier: the flip is revenue-per-token below infrastructure amortization sustained ~2–3 quarters at scale (gauge GV14), triggering the B1 fragility ceiling. What insulates the base case from the bust is the same thing that saved the fiber *incumbents*: balance sheet. The hyperscalers self-fund from **$50B+/yr free cash flow**; the SPV-financed neocloud tier does not, and that is where the impairment marks land first.

### The slow clock: Dalio, and the monetary envelope

The Perez surge is nested inside a Dalio Big Cycle in its **late-top / early-decline** phase for the incumbent reserve power. This is the boundary condition that sets the cost of capital and the geopolitical stakes, and it must not be averaged into the tech cycle — it *bounds* it. The debt-service arithmetic and reserve-currency drift push toward soft financial repression: r* upward-sticky, the dollar's reserve share ~58% and softening, Treasury-plumbing stress latent (loop B4). The historical rhyme is the sterling handover of 1890–1914 and the incumbent's temptation to inflate away obligations rather than default. Crucially, Dalio's cycle supplies the *hope* that resolves the debt: a productivity miracle that lets deficits self-liquidate (gauge GV40). That is the shared hinge — if the AI TFP offset arrives contemporaneously with the debt peak, the late-cycle resolves benignly; if it arrives late (capex is contemporaneous, measured TFP is not), repression is the path. The empire-cycle backdrop is also why the *second pole* matters: this is a contested-hegemony transition, and the chip/mineral/power chokepoints are the arenas of that contest.

### The fast clock: the decisive-lead reference classes (and the nuclear singularity)

The quarantined tail — RSI takeoff freezing the field into winner-take-all (Branch B, ~0.10) — has exactly one clean historical analogue for a *crossover*, and it is instructive precisely because it is so rare. **Nuclear criticality (1945)** is the only genuine physical-threshold discontinuity in the corpus: below critical mass, nothing; above it, a chain reaction. It produced a real but *brief* monopoly. And the reason it was brief is the second lesson: **Klaus Fuchs, Theodore Hall and the Manhattan espionage network compressed the Soviet timeline from an estimated ~8 years to ~4** — the bomb *diffused through human vectors* almost immediately. The US "unassailable lead" of 1945 was gone by 1949. This is why our takeoff analysis puts P(unassailable | takeoff) at only **0.12**: even granting the crossover, the lead leaks unless weights, methods, and talent do *not* diffuse — and today's diffusion clock runs at a **3-month** lag and closing [chart: gt3_master_derivative.png].

The other decisive-lead channels in the corpus are all *bounded* rent, not world-domination:

- **Standard Oil** cornered a *physical* resource (pipelines, rail rebates) — took decades, produced coercive economic power, and was broken by antitrust. Real lock-in, bounded outcome.
- **Google Search / Wintel** — data-and-revenue flywheels that genuinely compounded and *persisted* — produced durable **market** leadership, never a singleton.
- **No singleton has ever formed.** Rome, Pax Britannica, the 1945 nuclear monopoly — the nearest approaches all decayed.

This is the empirical spine of the R1-vs-R3 verdict: the mechanisms that could lock an AI lead are individually weak against diffusion, because returns to software self-improvement look constant-to-diminishing, not explosive. METR RE-bench shows AI at **~4x human experts on 2-hour ML-R&D tasks but ~0.5x at 32 hours** — sub-unity and *stuck* at the long horizon that would matter for recursive self-improvement [chart: gate_GV11.png]. Every RSI gauge reads sub-unity; every diffusion gauge compounds (algorithmic efficiency halving ~every 8 months, open-weight token share **4%→33%** 2023→late-2025). The nuclear analogy tells us that even a *real* crossover diffuses; the semiconductor-learning-curve analogy (Intel compounding a process lead) tells us that absent increasing returns the gap stays fixed-multiplicative, not exploding. Hence quarantine, not dismissal: hold Branch B strictly apart at 0.10, watch the RE-bench 32h+ crossing above all else.

### The chokepoint reference classes (why the moat holds at *training* and leaks at *outcomes*)

The buildout-bust and decisive-lead classes are the arc; the chokepoint classes explain the terrain. Two litho analogies calibrate the R4 export-control moat. **Nikon/Canon vs ASML (1984–2020)** and the **Carl Zeiss/Schott precision-optics ecosystem** are the strongest priors that leading-edge lithography cannot be closed on a short clock — the tacit-knowledge optical ecosystem took ASML three decades to build and even WWII forced-transfer failed to replicate Zeiss. This grounds the committed call that ASML holds **~100% of sub-5nm EUV tooling through 2028**, and that a captured TSMC would be a *non-functional* asset. **COCOM → Wassenaar → BIS (1949–present)** prices the control regime at buying **~5–10 years** of frontier-training delay — the Entity List has grown **236 (2016) → ~1,300 (2025)**, NVIDIA's China revenue share fell **26%→13%**. But the **VoIP/Skype-vs-PTT** analogy is the disanalogy that bites: a goods-based control regime is structurally defeated on the *services* leg — inference-as-a-service and open weights leak the *outcome* even as the hardware is denied. Leading-edge chips bind **training, not inference.** So the moat can succeed at the frontier while the paradigm "fails" on diffusion and value-capture simultaneously. Meanwhile the **2010 China rare-earth embargo on Japan** is the template for B3: the mineral weapon is short and reversible (gallium **$298/kg (2020) → $2,269 (2025)**) but durably accelerates de-risking — a lever, not a wall.

The buildout ceiling has its own priors. The **Alcoa aluminum energy-geography lock-in (1886–1945)** predicts compute migrates to cheap power; the fiber glut warns the **90 GW-by-2030** data-center load forecast may itself be the overshoot. The US power bind is real and binding *now*: interconnection queue ~2,600 GW, request-to-COD **60 months**, HV transformer lead time **12mo (2018) → 40mo (2026)**, against China adding **540 GW/yr** [chart: gt2_us_power_bind.png]. And the **Liberty Ship / Kaiser crash-ramp** sets the floor on how fast denial can be beaten — but only under war-economy mobilization, which is the disanalogy: absent a Taiwan fracture (Branch D, ~0.15, the one discontinuity that zeroes both poles), nobody is on a war footing.

### What transfers, and what does not

The transferable lessons: **(1)** frenzy buildouts resolve through a turning-point wobble, not a smooth deployment — expect a partial shakeout; **(2)** the asset outlives its financiers and rent migrates up-stack — the frontier-builder is often not the value-winner; **(3)** decisive leads are real but bounded and *diffuse* — 1945 became 1949; **(4)** tacit-knowledge manufacturing chokepoints (optics, single-crystal turbine blades, EUV) close on multi-decade clocks, if at all.

The disanalogies that make this genuinely novel — and why it is a *chokepoint lattice against two clocks*, not a single race: no prior surge ran the **diffusion clock (3-month lag, 40x/yr price decline) simultaneously with a decisive-capability race**; no prior chokepoint regime had a **services escape-valve** that leaks outcomes while denying goods; and no prior debt-cycle top had a candidate **productivity miracle** as its own potential resolution. The history says the modal path is messy, multipolar, up-stack deployment under soft repression — with the physical-frontier winner frequently not the value winner — overhung by two discrete detonators that the reference classes tell us to hold apart rather than blend away.

### 6. Where our confidence is honestly bounded

Three places, and a Bridgewater panel should press hardest here.

**First, the takeoff discontinuity is unfalsifiable in advance.** Our quarantine of B rests on the claim that returns to AI ML-R&D are r≤1 and the doubling-count stays *constant* relative to diffusion. But the fast-takeoff crossover (we score it P=0.25 *conditional on* takeoff) is "internally valid but load-bearing on an unproven discontinuity." We will not see criticality until we are past it. This is why we quarantine rather than average — a 0.10 branch with unbounded, field-freezing payoff must not be blended into a modal shape. If the panel disagrees anywhere, it will be on whether 0.10 is too low; our defense is that *every* RSI gauge reads sub-unity and stuck at long horizons *today*, and P(unassailable|takeoff)=0.12 even if it fires.

**Second, we are timing a race with two clocks whose relative speed we infer, not measure.** "Decisive threshold before commoditization completes, pre-2032" is a horse-race between an accelerating numerator (capability) and a compounding denominator (diffusion). We are confident diffusion leads *now*; we are far less confident about the *derivative of the gap* three years out. The nuclear "too-cheap-to-meter" negative-learning-curve analogy cuts against naive extrapolation of scaling returns — but so does it cut against naive extrapolation of diffusion.

**Third, B1 is latent, and latent is not safe.** We call the base case (0.55) over the bust (0.20), but hyperscaler FCF insulation is exactly the balance-sheet strength that let the fiber buildout run two years past the point of no return before 2000–02. Our probability split assumes the SPV/neocloud tier cracks *before* it drags the insulated core; if circular vendor-financing has knitted them together more tightly than we model, C is underweight. The honest statement: the *sign* of our forecast (up-stack rent migration, physical-frontier winner ≠ value winner) is high-conviction; the *timing* of the turning-point test is where we would revise first and fastest.
