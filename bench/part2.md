# The Binding Constraint Is Electrons and Jurisdiction, Not Intelligence

Frontier AI is real and still scaling, but its power is gated by grids, fabs, and jurisdiction — not by who reaches AGI first. Modern mercantilism (CHIPS Act, IRA, China's Big Fund, export controls) manufactures a capex prisoner's dilemma that elevates r* and forces a physical buildout no actor can will into existence — so AI enriches and displaces without handing anyone a decisive lead, and the West's containment tools carry a built-in expiry date.

## The Mercantilist Capex Trap

Start with the subsidy. Industrial policy — the CHIPS Act, the IRA, the EU Green Deal, China's semiconductor fund — turned compute into a strategic good that states pay to onshore. Once every bloc subsidizes capacity, no player can stand down: to stand down is to cede the frontier, so all build at once. That is a prisoner's dilemma, and its output is a capex wave that runs ahead of the physical inputs it needs. Capability scales (D1), but the buildout is gated by grid interconnection, transformers, fabs, and dense-cluster silicon that diffuse on decade timelines, not software timelines (D3). The result is not a shortage that clears — it is a persistent bottleneck. Expect at least one announced 1 GW-plus US data center to slip publicly (F1, P=0.87): the grid, not the GPU order book, is binding.

This subsidized, capital-heavy, leaky buildout has two macro consequences. First, competing to fund it lifts the equilibrium real rate — capital is bid for physical scarcity. Second, because the base model commoditizes as compute scales and leaks, rent migrates up-stack to distribution and proprietary context (D2); the cheapest API price for GPT-4-class capability collapses (F2, P=0.88). No durable model moat forms. Meanwhile the productivity payoff arrives too late to grow out of sovereign debt, so the state reaches for its oldest tool — financial repression. Negative US real rates for a sustained stretch (F8, P=0.55) are the debt-management instrument, not an accident.

## The Consensus Is Fighting the Last Layer

Export controls are the sharpest mercantilist instrument the West holds — and they are aimed at the wrong layer. BIS licensing, CFIUS, and siting rules target silicon and models. But value already migrated up-stack to distribution (D2), and physical control is migrating off the regulable grid. Controls leak: expect 100,000-plus restricted chips documented inside China in a single year (F5, P=0.65). A chokepoint that leaks and captures no rent is a chokepoint with an expiry date.

The wildcard the consensus does not price is space-compute. SpaceX/xAI's Colossus-class deployments plus Starlink point to frontier compute that builds faster than hyperscaler norms, sources power off the constrained grid, and is jurisdiction-agnostic. A 200 MW-equivalent frontier deployment operating primarily off-grid (F4, P=0.62) simultaneously eases the physical bottleneck, routes around BIS/CFIUS/siting, and adds a buildout node that makes the silicon chokepoint leak faster. Containment premised on controlling where compute physically sits fails once compute stops needing a jurisdiction.

This is why "who wins AGI" is the wrong question. Trade-order fragmentation (T4) is the container inside which two loosely-coupled compute stacks form — but consolidation here is economic gravity, not strategic lock-in. Independent frontier labs get absorbed into distribution platforms (F10, P=0.50) because rent sits there, not in weights. The world splits into two stacks with no stable middle, yet neither confers a game-closing military edge. Powerful GPT, not a singularity: pervasive economic impact and labor displacement, with a decisive lead denied to everyone.

## What to Watch, Bet, and Fear

Watch the grid, not the leaderboard. Interconnection queues, transformer lead times, and power-purchase agreements are the leading indicators of AI's real trajectory; model benchmarks are noise. Watch swing states: expect at least three of the twenty most populous non-aligned states to adopt predominantly Chinese AI stacks by 2030 (F12, P=0.45) — supply-chain weaponization and financing, not model quality, decide alignment.

Bet on the physical and the up-stack. Own power, grid access, and distribution channels; underwrite the arbitrageurs of jurisdiction. Fade the durable-model-moat trade and the pure-play frontier lab — F2 and F10 say that rent bleeds out. Own duration into financial repression: negative real rates (F8) are policy, and Treasuries remain the funding haven for the capex cycle even as they are debased in real terms. US–China goods trade holding near 80% of prior levels (F15, P=0.62) tells you interdependence persists — it does not defuse the tail.

Fear labor displacement outrunning the institutions meant to cushion it: white-collar AI-exposed employment falls in at least one major occupation (F7, P=0.55), and backlash arrives reactively, after bargaining power has fragmented (D4→D5). And fear the Taiwan tail (F11, P=0.12) — low-probability, but economic interdependence does not price it out.

---

*The race everyone is watching — who reaches AGI first — is not the race that matters. The binding constraints are electrons and jurisdiction, and the actor who controls power and platforms, not weights, captures whatever lead there is to capture.*
