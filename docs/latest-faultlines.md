# The Fault-Line Forecasts — 10 Predictions That Test the Cruxes

## Design principle

These are not confirmations of the base world; they are **discriminating tests**. Each of the ten is chosen because its resolution *moves you between worlds* on the crux map (`latest-cruxmap.md`): one direction leaves you in the base world ("electrons and jurisdiction; no decisive lead"), the other drops you into a specific, named alternative. Each probability is **weighted from three inputs** — the crux resolution and its confidence tag, the historical analogy base rate (#A-series), and the topic notes (#T-series) for and against — and each names *the tell*: the single leading signal that resolves it early. The set is built to span all five independent/semi-independent axes (capability, constraint, rent-layer, institutions, macro) plus the labor leg and the geopolitical container, so that collectively the ten answer the only question that matters for calibration: **which of the seven worlds are we actually in?**

Read each as: *base world says X at probability P; if it resolves ¬X, you are in World Y and the trade inverts.*

## P1 — The grid binds: a US 1GW+ project slips on power — **P = 0.85**
**Statement:** By end-2028, ≥1 announced US AI data-center project of ≥1GW is publicly delayed, downsized, or cancelled with grid-interconnection or power supply cited as a primary reason (company statement, regulatory filing, or major-outlet reporting).
**Crux tested:** T6-C4 (yes/med) — the binding constraint is physical inputs. The load-bearing pivot of the whole thesis.
**Weighting:** High base rate — interconnection queues, transformer lead times, and permitting gaps are already the gating factor (#T6-3, #T6-17, #T6-7, #T6-11; #A5 US nuclear execution, #A9 Apollo execution-quality). Discounted only slightly because the near-certainty makes it *diagnostically weak alone* (a slip can be caused by demand-softening or an efficiency shock, not the grid — the World B / World C reading). **The discriminating version** — a slip caused by the grid *while interconnection queues are still lengthening* — is nearer **P=0.60**.
**The tell:** direction of interconnection queue times and transformer lead times through 2027. Lengthening → base world; shortening → World B.
**Flips to:** World B (efficiency) or World F (asymmetry) if the slip is demand-driven or if China's clusters complete on schedule while the US slips.

## P2 — Efficiency shock relaxes the grid before the buildout completes — **P = 0.40**
**Statement:** By end-2027, a frontier-class model (≥GPT-4-class on standard public benchmarks) is trained for <10% of the compute of the prior frontier leader, AND aggregate frontier training-compute demand growth visibly decelerates (interconnection queue times shorten, or a major hyperscaler cuts a previously-announced multi-GW plan citing efficiency/demand rather than supply).
**Crux tested:** T6-C4 and T9-C4 (both) — is the binding margin physical or algorithmic?
**Weighting:** The compute-side alone is *likely* (~0.70) — DeepSeek V3/R1 at <10% expected compute and ~$6M R1 already exist (#T2-3, #T6-93, #T6-13); controls actively *incentivize* this research (#T9-95). But the load-bearing rider is the **demand-deceleration** — Jevons rebound (#T3-8) and inference-time scaling (#T2-2) plausibly offset per-unit gains and keep aggregate demand rising. So the conjunction that actually relaxes the grid is nearer **0.40**.
**The tell:** a <10%-compute frontier model *and* queue times shortening in the same window.
**Flips to:** **World B (Efficiency/DeepSeek)** — this kills the *title* ("electrons") while *strengthening* the conclusion ("no decisive lead," because cheaper compute diffuses faster). The single most important disconfirmer of the headline.

## P3 — No developer holds a durable capability lead — **P = 0.66**
**Statement:** Through end-2029, on a standard aggregate public benchmark, no single developer's lead over the 5th-ranked frontier model ever exceeds 12 months (time-lag to matching score).
**Crux tested:** T9-C1 (no/med) and T9-C2 — decisive capability advantage is not coherent/durable. The other pivot.
**Weighting:** Strong diffusion base rate — major military-relevant tech diffuses in 5–10yr (#T9-4), a leader's revealed architecture accelerates followers (#T9-28), the Manhattan monopoly lasted only ~4yr to Joe-1 (#A3), and API deflation + open-weight replication show leads bleeding fast (#T2-23). Held below high because a decisive *transient* lead of 1–4yr is fully consistent with these base rates (the unbounded seam) and recursive self-improvement (T3 #27) would break the follower-catch-up assumption.
**The tell:** a sustained >18-month lead that open-weights and distillation demonstrably cannot close; or demonstrated recursive self-improvement.
**Flips to:** **World A (AGI race / winner-take-all)** — inverts the entire thesis, not one section.

## P4 — The model layer commoditizes: API price collapses — **P = 0.85**
**Statement:** By end-2028, the cheapest per-token API price for a GPT-4-class-or-better model is ≥90% below the cheapest such price in January 2026.
**Crux tested:** T2-C2 (no/med) + T2-C3 (yes/med) — open-source + distillation close the gap; rent migrates up-stack.
**Weighting:** Near-direct extrapolation of observed ~100x API deflation 2023–2025 (#T2-23), distillation as a one-way ratchet (#T2-25), open-weight releases at zero marginal price (#T2-6; #A6 Netscape→Chrome, #A8 Nucor/EAF disruption-from-below). The single highest-conviction forecast in the set.
**The tell:** if price deflation *halts* while NVIDIA/ASML gross margins *rise* — that is the signature of a chokepoint capturing rent rather than commoditizing (see P5). Falling price + falling chokepoint margin = base world; stalling price + rising chokepoint margin = World D.
**Flips to:** confirms base; its *stall* is the leading tell for World D.

## P5 — The tooling chokepoint holds: no volume leading-edge without EUV — **P = 0.62**
**Statement:** Through end-2030, China does NOT reach commercial-yield volume production of ≤5nm-class logic without ASML EUV (i.e., EUV/EDA tooling remains a binding chokepoint on indigenous leading-edge capacity).
**Crux tested:** T7-C4 (no/high in the essay — but this prediction tests the *counter*) — is the durable chokepoint at the tooling layer?
**Weighting:** This is the deliberate counter-weight to the thesis's "no durable moat" claim. ASML EUV is a tighter chokepoint than TSMC itself (#T9-25, #T6-26), CUDA is a 10+yr geography-independent moat (#T6-27), HBM (#T6-15) and packaging (#T6-14) and EDA are oligopolies that don't leak like finished chips. Base rate favors the chokepoint *holding* through 2030 (~0.62) even as chip-flow controls leak (P6). The essay's own concession that rent is captured at EUV/HBM/packaging means it already half-believes this.
**The tell:** credible reporting of indigenous EUV or DUV-multipatterning ≤5nm at commercial yield and scale.
**Flips to:** **World D (Containment holds)** — containment has no expiry date; it just moved upstream; the West holds a durable, geography-independent strategic lever. Directly contradicts the essay's headline "controls expire."

## P6 — Chip-flow controls leak: 100k+ restricted chips reach China — **P = 0.65**
**Statement:** By end-2028, credible reporting documents ≥100,000 restricted advanced accelerators (H100-class or better) entering China in a single calendar year via smuggling, transshipment, or offshore access.
**Crux tested:** T8-C3 (no/med) — export controls erode from within and leak.
**Weighting:** Base rate for technology-denial regimes is leakage that raises cost without stopping diffusion (#A6 COCOM slowed-not-stopped; #T9-15 third-country leakage; #T8-3 diffusion rule eroded by commercial pressure). Note P6 and P5 together encode the thesis's honest position: **chip-flow leaks (P6 high) but tooling may not (P5 holds)** — the split the essay blurs.
**The tell:** documented single-year volume through third-country hubs.
**Flips to:** if <100k and China is compute-starved at the frontier, controls are enforceable at the chip layer too — reprice F5/F12 and weaken the "expiry date" claim.

## P7 — Negative real rates arrive (repression path) — **P = 0.55**
**Statement:** By end-2029, the US real policy rate (effective fed funds − 12-month headline CPI) is negative for ≥6 consecutive months.
**Crux tested:** T1-C1 (no/med) + T1-C2 (no/med) — productivity doesn't outrun the debt; CB independence erodes.
**Weighting:** Fiscal-dominance and repression precedents are strong (#T1-2, #T1-9 WWII repression, #A1 1941-51 YCC, #A7 MEFO bills). But 0.55 is honest and *deliberately low* because the number blends two opposite regimes reaching the same sign (repression vs. World C's deflationary bust) minus the de-anchoring path that flips the sign (#T1-20). **This is a calibration warning, not a conviction:** the sign is likely, the *mechanism* is a coin-flip (see P8).
**The tell:** the Fed holding real rates *positive* through a genuine debt-service squeeze without yield caps or monetization → World E.
**Flips to:** **World E (Goldilocks)** if TFP accelerates >1pp above trend (#T1-16) and rates stay positive — the system grows out of the debt and repression is never needed.

## P8 — An AI-capex credit bust cracks the cycle — **P = 0.38**
**Statement:** Before end-2028, a GPU-financing or data-center credit stress event occurs — a major hyperscaler/neocloud GPU-fleet writedown, a data-center REIT or private-credit default tied to AI capex, or a circular-vendor-financing unwind — accompanied by 10-year real yields *falling* while credit spreads *widen*.
**Crux tested:** T1-C3 (yes/med) — in a bust, negative real rates come from collapsing nominal rates, not repression.
**Weighting:** Lower probability but the notes weight the mechanism heavily: fiber-overbuild overshoot (#T6-8, #A4 dot-com fiber), Perez installation-phase crash (T3 #56), GPU-as-collateral margin-call cascade (#T6-36), circular vendor financing (#T6-35), Fisher debt-deflation (#T1-34, #T1-68). ~0.38 reflects that this is a tail, not the base case — but it is the tail that *inverts the headline trade*.
**The tell:** the first hyperscaler/neocloud writedown or DC-linked default; real yields falling *with spreads widening* (deflationary haven bid, not managed repression).
**Flips to:** **World C (Debt-deflation)** — "own duration into repression" becomes "own quality/cash into deflation." Same F8 sign as P7, opposite regime, opposite trade. Frequently *triggered by* P2 (efficiency strands the overbuild).

## P9 — White-collar displacement shows up in the data — **P = 0.55**
**Statement:** By end-2029, US BLS occupational data shows a ≥10% decline from the 2024-25 peak in employment for at least one major AI-exposed white-collar occupation (e.g., software developers, paralegals, customer-service representatives).
**Crux tested:** T3-C1 (no/**low**) + T3-C3 (no/med) — weak reinstatement; AI crosses from tool to substitute. The softest load-bearing leg (low confidence).
**Weighting:** Supported by visible junior knowledge-worker hiring softness (#T3-7), agentic AI closing the adaptive escape hatch (#T3-3), and the Wapping delayed-then-sudden template (#A8). Held at 0.55 with T3-C1 at *low* confidence — appropriately humble — because enterprise adoption lag (#T3-9) and demographic shortages absorbing displacement (#T3-16) genuinely cut the other way, and displacement-without-diffusion must coexist with slow aggregate TFP (the F7↔T1-C1 tension).
**The tell:** a single high-salience agentic catastrophe triggering an early insurance/liability clampdown (#T3-83, #T7-42) — backlash then arrives *early via the safety frame*, not late via the jobs frame, inverting the D4→D5 sequencing.
**Flips to:** techno-optimist absorption (new complementary tasks, #T3-21) weakens it; a catastrophe-clampdown changes its *timing and channel*.

## P10 — The buildout binds asymmetrically: China outpaces the US — **P = 0.50**
**Statement:** Through end-2028, China's AI-relevant power/compute buildout outpaces the US's — operationally, ≥1 Chinese multi-GW AI cluster comes online broadly on schedule while a comparable US project slips (P1), and China's new generation capacity feeding data centers grows materially faster than the US's.
**Crux tested:** the axis-B *asymmetry* modifier (#T5-56 ~150 GW/yr, #T5-57 ~27 reactors under construction vs. decade-plus US nuclear licensing, #T5-54) and T9-C3 (Taiwan).
**Weighting:** ~0.50 for the asymmetry *materializing visibly* — the generation-rate gap is real and documented, but "outpaces" requires the Chinese clusters to actually deliver frontier-relevant compute, which the tooling chokepoint (P5) may throttle. This is **the smallest and most dangerous flip**: it needs no capability discontinuity, only a fact the essay already concedes.
**The tell:** Chinese multi-GW clusters completing on schedule *with frontier-relevant chips* while US 1GW+ projects slip.
**Flips to:** **World F (China-asymmetric lead)** — "no decisive lead" becomes "a decisive lead exists, and it is China's"; the *title* survives, the *thesis* dies.

**Paired tail (standing, not a discriminator):** PLA declared blockade/quarantine/kinetic action against Taiwan by end-2028 — **P = 0.12**. Low base rate, but economic interdependence does not price it out (T9-C3 no/low; #T9-2, #A1 oil-embargo→Pearl Harbor use-it-or-lose-it). Seizure not destruction is the coherent strategy (#T9-10); seized fabs may not function without foreign inputs (#T9-13), capping the payoff but not the probability. This is the highest-consequence node in the set and reprices sharply upward inside World A or World F if a decisive-transient lead matures in the 2027–2032 window.

## How the ten resolve the map

- **Base world confirmed** if: P1 hits *with lengthening queues*, P3 holds, P4 collapses *with falling chokepoint margins*, P6 leaks, P7 negative *via accommodation*, and P2/P5/P8/P10 all fail. This is the {diffusing · physical · up-stack · erode · repression} corner — own power, own up-stack, own duration.
- **You have left the base world** the moment **three of {P2, P5, P8, P10, and a P3-breaking sustained lead}** print together. That is the disconfirming-scenario threshold from `latest-dense.md`, re-expressed as live forecasts:
  - P2 + P8 firing together → **Worlds B→C** (efficiency shock strands the overbuild; own cash, not power or duration).
  - P5 + P3-break → **Worlds D→A** (durable tooling chokepoint + durable capability lead; own ASML/NVIDIA and the frontier lab).
  - P10 + P1 → **World F** (the asymmetry is the lead; the title was right and the thesis was wrong).
- **The calibration honesty check:** P7 and P8 reach the *same sign* on real rates through *opposite mechanisms* and demand *opposite trades*. Any forecast set that reports only "negative real rates, P=0.55" without splitting P7 from P8 is hiding the single most consequential fork in the macro book.

# Part B — Base-case predictions: what happens if the base world holds

The ten fault-lines above tell you when you have *left* the base world. These base-case predictions tell you what to *expect while you are still in it* — the concrete, dated things that populate the {diffusing · physical · up-stack · erode · repression} corner. They were mined from the full note stack (`t1`–`t9` notes + conclusions) via six parallel note-mining passes, one per driver cluster, and each is grounded in specific note codes and a named historical analogy. Where a base-case prediction coincides with an original submission forecast, the F-code is noted; several deliberately re-incorporate the forecasts that were *dropped* from the 10-forecast submission (F6, F9, F10, F12, F13, F15), because as base-case *expectations* — rather than crux *discriminators* — they carry real information. Probabilities are spread 0.48–0.78 and are lower than the prose confidence, by design: the base world is a ~0.30 joint bet, so its constituent predictions should sit in the 0.55–0.75 band, not at 0.9.

## D7 · Macro — fiscal dominance leans to repression

**BC1 — Interest-cost cliff.** US federal net interest reaches ≥25% of federal revenue by end-2028 as 2020–21 near-zero issuance rolls into higher rates; the term premium rises ≥100bp off 2024. **P=0.65.** Grounds: #T1-16 (duration cliff), #T1-2 (interest already ~20% of revenue), #T1-14 (temporal mismatch); analogy: **1941–51 WWII roll-over structure**. Discount: #T1-25 dollar absorbed record issuance post-2022 — absorptive capacity is real.

**BC2 — Bottleneck inflation, not broad inflation.** Data-center power, AI-adjacent real estate, and high-voltage-electrical-engineer wages compound 15–30%/yr through 2029, staying ≥5pp above headline CPI. **P=0.72.** Grounds: #T1-8 (sectorally-concentrated bottlenecks), #T1-13 (AI/energy-transition capex collision), #T1-98 (transmission decouples generation from delivered cost); analogy: **Meiji/zaibatsu state-directed capex sectoral inflation**. Discount: #T1-4 tech waves are net-deflationary; #T1-99 Jevons.

**BC3 — Dollar net-extends under soft yield management.** USD reserve share drifts to ~55–57% by end-2028 while dollar stablecoins grow to $0.5–1T (net footprint *extends*), and the Fed maintains de facto yield management (resists ≥3 consecutive hikes into fiscal stress) without formal YCC. **P=0.60.** Grounds: #T1-49 (stablecoins as price-insensitive T-bill buyer), #T1-54 (dollarization-2.0), #T1-11 (no statutory independence armor), #T1-73 (SLR/dealer plumbing compels intervention); analogy: **Marshall-Plan → Eurodollar offshore expansion; 1942–51 Fed YCC**. Discount: #T1-7 weaponization accelerates diversification; Fed *did* hike hard 2022–23. (Macro anchor F8 negative-real-rates is fault-line **P7**.)

## D3 · Physical constraint binds and diffuses slowly

**BC4 — The queue stays clogged.** The US FERC interconnection queue stays >2,000GW with median energization waits of 5–7yr through 2028; ≥1 grid operator (PJM/ERCOT/MISO/Dominion/Georgia Power) imposes a *new* large-load moratorium, queue freeze, or dedicated data-center tariff; ≥2 hyperscaler projects are delayed citing interconnection. **P=0.75.** Grounds: #T6-3, #T6-17, #T6-60, #T6-64, #T6-72; analogy: **US nuclear-buildout / Apollo execution-quality**. Discount: FERC Order 2023 + state pre-emption (#T6-67/68) can shorten queues faster than extrapolated. (=reinstated **F13**.)

**BC5 — Packaging, not the node, is the bind.** CoWoS / advanced packaging (not leading-edge node capacity) is the binding accelerator constraint for >80% of H/B-series production, with 18–24-month lead times through 2028. **P=0.68.** Grounds: #T6-14 (CoWoS is the actual bottleneck), #T6-15 (HBM oligopoly), #T6-102 (packaging is the crux layer); analogy: **picks-and-shovels / semiconductor packaging historically independent of node maturity**. Discount: Samsung/Micron HBM scale-up + chiplet substitutes.

**BC6 — The buildout asymmetry is a fact (even if the lead isn't).** China completes ~3× US new generation capacity annually through 2028 and brings ≥4 new reactors online while the US brings ~0 new large reactors online. **P=0.75.** Grounds: #T5-56 (~150GW/yr), #T5-57 (~27 reactors under construction vs decade-plus US licensing); analogy: **electrification-era grid buildout**. Discount: US behind-the-meter gas + solar ramp. (The *raw asymmetry* is near-certain here; whether it *translates into a decisive lead* is the stronger fault-line **P10 / World F**.)

## D2 · Model layer commoditizes; rent sits up-stack AND at the tooling layer

**BC7 — Open-weight gap compresses.** The open-weight frontier gap narrows to ≤6–9 months on agentic benchmarks (SWE-bench Verified, GAIA) by end-2028, with a DeepSeek-class distilled model within ~30% of the prior closed frontier. **P=0.64.** Grounds: #T2-3 (DeepSeek efficiency shock), #T2-6 (Llama-405B near-GPT-4), #T2-25 (distillation ratchet, no mode-collapse); analogy: **Nucor EAF disruption-from-below**. Discount: #T2-21 the gap stays large on exactly the high-WTP agentic tasks.

**BC8 — Rent is captured at the tooling layer.** NVIDIA + hyperscaler-owned silicon consolidate >80% of frontier training/premium inference through end-2028 and NVIDIA gross margin holds >50% — i.e., durable rent sits at CUDA/compute, *not* the weights. **P=0.70.** Grounds: #T7-C4 (compute chokepoint durable, high-conf), #T7-126 (CUDA middleware monopoly); analogy: **Wintel ISA/ABI; ASML EUV 30-yr durability**. This is the base-case's honest concession — and the exact reading that, if it *also* captures the value share (margins *rising* as API prices fall), tips into fault-line **P5 / World D**. (API price collapse F2 is fault-line **P4**.)

**BC9 — EU AI Act dilutes.** The EU materially weakens/delays/exempts GPAI/foundation-model obligations; enforcement produces <$50M aggregate fines on non-EU providers through 2028; geo-fencing (selective per-region feature withholding), not global compliance, becomes the operational mode. **P=0.70.** Grounds: #T7-C3, #T7-76 (broken forcing function), #T7-95 (Draghi/digital-omnibus rollback live); analogy: **GDPR under-enforcement (4–7yr, sub-0.1%-revenue fines)**. (=reinstated **F6**.)

**BC10 — A frontier lab gets absorbed.** ≥1 independent top-tier lab (OpenAI/Anthropic/xAI/Mistral or comparable) is acquired by or folded under majority/exclusive operational control of a hyperscaler/platform by end-2029. **P=0.52.** Grounds: #T2-5 (model-layer value arbitraged), #T7 lab-economics; analogy: **browser-era consolidation (Netscape→platform)**. Discount: sovereign/price-insensitive capital (#T8-2, #T1-21) can keep labs independent. (=reinstated **F10**.)

**BC11 — Insurance becomes the adoption gate.** In regulated verticals (health, finance, autonomous systems), insurance/liability — not regulatory compliance — becomes the dominant AI adoption gate by end-2028, with insurers setting technical requirements exceeding regulatory thresholds. **P=0.60.** Grounds: #T7-42 (insurers as de facto standards body), #T7-C6, #T7-111 (product-liability doctrine); analogy: **post-Three-Mile-Island nuclear safety-cost moat**.

## D4 · Labor displaces faster than it reinstates

**BC12 — Entry-level hiring compresses first.** Entry-level hiring in named BLS occupations (Paralegals 23-2011, Programmers 15-1251, Financial Analysts 13-2051) trails the 2020–23 baseline by ≥15pp y/y by end-2028, and ≥2 named large firms announce ≥15% entry-level headcount cuts explicitly attributing them to agentic AI. **P=0.66.** Grounds: #T3-7 (junior-hiring leading indicator already visible), #T3-92/94 (cohort-depletion / apprenticeship-pipeline severing); analogy: **CAD-draftsmen 1990s; spreadsheet-clerk attrition**. Discount: #T3-9 enterprise adoption lag. (The broader occupation −10% is fault-line **P9 / F7**.)

**BC13 — Backlash arrives via the safety frame, not the jobs frame.** By end-2029 the primary political vehicle for AI-diffusion throttling is a safety/liability bill (FTC/FDA/NHTSA framing, trial-bar + consumer-advocacy coalition), *not* a labor-protection bill (DOL/NLRB). **P=0.60.** Grounds: #T3-91 (safety frame outcompetes jobs frame in polling), #T3-86 (trial bar > organized labor), #T3-83 (one-catastrophe tipping point); analogy: **SOX post-Enron (investor/audit-liability frame, not labor)**.

## D6 / D1 · Container fragments atop persistent flows; controls slow-not-stop; live tail

**BC14 — Trade reroutes, it does not repatriate.** 2028 US-China two-way goods trade stays ≥80% of 2024; the adjustment is rerouting (Mexico/Vietnam up-chain, high Chinese input content) not repatriation (CHIPS/IRA *deployed* capex modest, net US AI/semis manufacturing jobs <100k); the WTO Appellate Body remains non-functional. **P=0.70.** Grounds: #T4-C1 (rule erosion atop flows), #T4-35 (Mexico relabelling), #T4-38 (record deficit *with* tariffs), #T4-C4/12 (AB paralysis); analogy: **Multi-Fiber Arrangement / US-Japan VER (bilateral falls, total climbs via third countries); GATT non-restoration**. (=reinstated **F15**.)

**BC15 — Controls buy years, not denial.** By end-2029 export controls read as "slow not stop": ≥100k restricted accelerators reach China in a year (F5) *and* China fields an indigenous sub-7nm accelerator (SMIC/Ascend-class) at ≥60% of then-frontier training capability. **P=0.62.** Grounds: #T9-94/95 (efficiency defeats hardware denial), #T9-22 (talent + transshipment), #T5-75 (SMIC 7nm via DUV); analogy: **COCOM slowed-not-stopped; crypto-wars/PGP leaked in weeks**. (Extends fault-line **P6 / F5**.)

**BC16 — The durable Western lever is the tool layer, not the chip.** Non-Taiwan sub-5nm logic stays <20% of global capacity through 2030 (Arizona at partial design capacity, Intel 18A low-volume, Rapidus delayed); TSMC retains ≥85% of frontier accelerator production; ASML/EUV/EDA remain the un-leaked chokepoint. **P=0.75.** Grounds: #T9-44 (2–3× cost cap on non-Taiwan viable capacity), #T9-39 (reshoring peaks *post*-window), #T5-4 (30–50% cost penalty structural), #T9-13 (seized fabs are shells without foreign inputs); analogy: **Chi-Chi 1999 → zero subsequent fab redundancy; Manhattan-era physical-scarcity moat**. (This is the base-case's version of **World D's** durable chokepoint — held here as compatible with "weights commoditize.")

**BC17 — Taiwan escalates in the gray zone, short of the declared tail.** By end-2030 China executes ≥1 gray-zone coercion (major exercise + harassment, subsea-cable severance, or sanctions escalation) that degrades TSMC export capacity 5–15% for 2–8 weeks, *without* triggering a US kinetic response. **P=0.58.** Grounds: #T9-27 (blockade/coercion is the un-deterred pathway), #T9-52 (cable severance below Article-5 threshold), #T9-36 (inventory-cliff makes small disruption asymmetric); analogy: **Crimea fait-accompli; Senkaku/SCS incremental coercion**. (Distinct from the *declared* blockade/kinetic tail F11 = **0.12**, the fault-line paired tail.)
